

## EXERCISE v01: The Boundary-Setting Script
---

---
Write down three requests you need to decline. Number them. Write your "no" for each one. Say each "no" aloud three times. Listen to your voice. Your voice is steady. Your voice is firm. Your voice is kind. You've practiced. You're ready. Tomorrow, use these words. Your script will hold you. Your preparation will carry you through.
---

## EXERCISE v02: The Pause Before Responding
---

---
Someone makes a request. Your instinct says yes. Stop. Count to ten slowly. Say: I need to check my capacity. Hang up or step back. Take five minutes. Ask yourself: do I want this. Do I have time for this. Is this my responsibility. The pause is your power. The pause creates choice. The choice is yours. Then you respond.
---

## EXERCISE v03: The Email Delay Tactic
---

---
Someone asks something via email. You want to say yes immediately. Don't send a response yet. Close the email. Wait four hours. Open it again. Read the request. What do you actually want to say. Write that. Don't rush the response. Your rushed response is usually yes. Your considered response is honest. Send the honest one. Your boundaries are in the delay.
---

## EXERCISE v04: The In-Person Boundary Setting
---

---
Someone makes a request in person. You feel pressure to answer immediately. Say: I need to think about this. Don't explain. Don't justify. Walking away is not rude. Walking away is necessary. Give yourself time to think. Return with your honest answer. Your time is your power. Your answer is your boundary.
---

## EXERCISE v05: The Calendar as Boundary
---

---
Open your calendar. Look at an empty hour. Block it for lunch. Actually write: lunch. Make it non-negotiable. Move meetings rather than losing lunch. Your lunch is a boundary. Your calendar shows your boundary. When someone wants to schedule over it, you point to your calendar. The calendar is objective. The calendar is your boundary made visible.
---

## EXERCISE v06: The Guilt Awareness Work
---

---
You're feeling guilty about your boundary. Sit with it. Feel the guilt in your body. Where is it. Notice it without changing it. Now ask: did I do something wrong. The answer is: no. I set a healthy boundary. The guilt is leftover from people-pleasing patterns. The guilt is not truth. The guilt will pass. Breathe. The boundary stays. The guilt fades.
---

## EXERCISE v07: The Physical Boundary Practice
---

---
In your office, stand with your arms at your sides. Slowly raise both arms to shoulder height. Your arms are creating space around you. Your arms are your boundary. Hold this for ten seconds. Feel the strength. Feel the space. Lower your arms. You just physically practiced your boundary. Your body knows it now. Your body can do this. Use this posture when setting boundaries.
---

## EXERCISE v08: The Boundary Question Work
---

---
Write down a request that made you uncomfortable. Under it, answer these questions: Am I responsible for this. Is this my role. Do I have capacity. What's the real cost to me. What would happen if I said no. Write your honest answers. Your answers show you the truth. The truth makes boundaries easier. You're not saying no from fear. You're saying no from truth.
---

## EXERCISE v09: The Stop-and-Redirect Technique
---

---
Someone interrupts your work. You want to help. Stop. Say: I'm focused on something right now. I can help you at three pm. Your boundary is the redirection. You're not saying no. You're saying: later. The person is helped. Your time is protected. Your boundary is both kind and firm.
---

## EXERCISE v10: The Value Clarification Work
---

---
Write down three of your core values. Family. Rest. Growth. Or others. Now answer: does this request align with my values. If yes, it's a genuine yes. If no, it's a genuine no. Your values are your boundaries made clear. Your values guide your yeses and nos. You're not being difficult. You're being true to what matters to you.
---

## EXERCISE v11: The Phone Boundary Practice
---

---
Someone calls during your offline time. You don't answer. Later you see the message. You respond the next day. You've set a boundary: I'm offline after six. You've kept the boundary: you didn't answer. You responded appropriately: the next working day. The boundary was respected. The relationship continued. Your time was protected.
---

## EXERCISE v12: The Boundary in Writing
---

---
Write out your boundary as a statement. Example: I work nine to five. I don't respond to emails after six. I take Saturdays off. Write this for each boundary you need. Write it in first person. Say it aloud. Your written boundary is clearer. Your stated boundary is stronger. Your boundary in your own voice is most powerful.
---

## EXERCISE v13: The Rising Tide Technique
---

---
Your boss keeps adding requests. You keep saying yes. Stop. Say to yourself: the tide is rising. Each yes is another wave. Soon I'll drown. I need to say no now. Saying no now protects me from drowning later. I'm not drowning. I'm standing firm. The tide rises around me. I remain standing. Your boundary is your solid ground.
---

## EXERCISE v14: The Meeting Boundary
---

---
A meeting is running over. You have another commitment. Look at the clock. Say: I need to step out. You don't explain. You don't apologize for your time. You stand. You excuse yourself. Your boundary was your other commitment. Your boundary was your integrity. You showed up when you said you would.
---

## EXERCISE v15: The Weekly Boundary Check-In
---

---
Every Friday, review your week. How many times did you say yes when you wanted to say no. How many times did you honor your boundaries. Where did you slip. Write this down. Don't judge. Just notice. Next week, practice one boundary more. One specific one. Each week, you strengthen your boundary muscle. You're getting better at this.
---

## EXERCISE v16: The Boundary Affirmation
---

---
Say these three times a day: My time is valuable. My energy is limited. My boundaries are healthy. Say them in the morning. Say them at noon. Say them at night. Your mind will believe what you repeat. Your mind will start to protect what you say is valuable. Your affirmations become your truth.
---

## EXERCISE v17: The Practice Conversation
---

---
In your car or office, say your boundary aloud. Practice saying no to requests. Hear yourself say it. Hear how it sounds. Is it kind. Is it firm. Practice until you feel comfortable. Your car is safe practice space. Your office with the door closed is safe. When the real conversation comes, you're ready. You've practiced. Your voice will be steady.
---

## EXERCISE v18: The Boundary Celebration
---

---
You set a boundary. You feel the guilt. Also feel this: you protected yourself. That's a win. Write it down. I set a boundary today. You're building evidence. Evidence that your boundaries are good. Evidence that you can do this. Celebrate every boundary. Your celebration reinforces the behavior.
---

## EXERCISE v19: The Boundary Visualization
---

---
Close your eyes. Visualize yourself saying no. See yourself calm. See yourself firm. See the person accepting it. See yourself relieved. Open your eyes. You've practiced this in your mind. Your mind knows it's possible. When it happens in real life, you've already done it once. Your visualization is your rehearsal.
---

## EXERCISE v20: The Boundary in Your Body
---

---
Stand with your feet wide. Plant them into the ground. Feel your strength. Feel your stability. Bring your hands to your heart. Say: I protect myself. Your body knows boundaries. Your body is a boundary. Your body stands firm. Feel this strength. Carry it with you. Your body remembers when you need it.
---

## EXERCISE v21: The Boundary With Your Boss
---

---
Write down what you need to protect: your time, your energy, your family time. Write what you'll do to protect it. Then arrange a meeting. Tell your boss: I want to discuss how we can make this work better for everyone. Propose your boundary. Your boundary isn't against them. It's for you. It's sustainable. They benefit from your sustainability.
---

## EXERCISE v22: The Slippery Slope Awareness
---

---
You said yes once. Now they ask more. You're on the slippery slope. You notice it. You stop. You say: I can't continue adding to my plate. I'm at capacity. You're not blaming them. You're stating facts. Your capacity is real. Your boundary is honest. Your slope is no longer slippery because you've stopped.
---

## EXERCISE v23: The Boundary Erosion Check
---

---
Your boundary used to be firm. Now it's softer. You're noticing the erosion. Stop it today. Rebuild your boundary. Make it clear again. Write it if needed. Tell the person if needed. Your boundary didn't fail. You got tired. Rest now. Rebuild tomorrow. Your boundaries are worth protecting. Protect them.
---

## EXERCISE v24: The No-Guilt Protocol
---

---
You said no. The guilt arrived. Follow the protocol: One. Notice the guilt. Two. Say: I did the right thing. Three. Breathe in calm. Four. Breathe out guilt. Five. Move forward. Don't sit in the guilt. Don't let guilt change your boundary. Move forward. The guilt will fade. Your boundary will remain.
---

## EXERCISE v25: The Boundary Witness
---

---
Tell someone about your boundary. Tell a friend. Tell a therapist. Say: I set this boundary. See them nod. See them approve. You're not alone in protecting yourself. Other people protect themselves too. You're normal. Your boundary is healthy. You're doing well. The witness confirms what you've done.
---

## EXERCISE v26: The Time-Limited Yes
---

---
Someone asks for your time. Instead of a permanent yes, offer: I can help for the next month. Then we'll revisit. Your boundary is time-limited. It's not forever. It's enough to help. It's also enough to protect your future. The person gets help. You get relief. Your boundary is kind and clear.
---

## EXERCISE v27: The Boundary Energy Check
---

---
Before you agree to something, check your energy. Do you have the energy for this. Say yes only if you do. Your energy is not infinite. Your boundary is protecting your finite energy. You can give from overflow. You cannot give from empty. Check your energy. Protect your energy. Your boundary is wise.
---

## EXERCISE v28: The Boundary Letter
---

---
Write a letter you never send. Write it to the person who taught you to say yes to everything. Write what you need them to know. Write how their pattern harmed you. Write your new boundary. Write that you're protecting yourself now. You don't send it. You've written it. You've released it. Your boundary is stronger.
---

## EXERCISE v29: The Boundary as Love
---

---
Your boundary is an act of love. Love for yourself. Love for others, actually. Because your sustainable boundaries mean you can show up better. You're protecting the energy you give. Your boundary is not selfish. Your boundary is wise. Your boundary is love. Believe this. Your boundary is good.

---
One month of boundary-setting. You've set three significant boundaries. Feel the difference. Feel your energy. You're less drained. You're more present. Your relationships are better because you're better. Your work is better because you're better. You're boundaries are good. Keep them. Strengthen them. They're working.
