# corporate_managers × burnout × the system running past empty
# Slot type: COMPRESSION
# Count: 30
# compression_family: C1–C5

---
## COMPRESSION v01
compression_family: C1
You've learned to run on fumes because stopping looks like failure. Your team needs you steady. Your confidence becomes the infrastructure they lean on. But infrastructure cracks from the inside first, and no one sees it until the weight shifts wrong. The emptiness is already there. You just haven't admitted it yet.
---
## COMPRESSION v02
compression_family: C2
Performance metrics don't measure the cost of keeping your own engine running while monitoring eight others. You moved into leadership because you were reliable. Reliable means you absorb the pressure that doesn't fit anywhere else. Now reliability is indistinguishable from running on empty, and you can't tell where the depletion ends and the dedication begins.
---
## COMPRESSION v03
compression_family: C3
The spreadsheet shows productivity. It doesn't show that you stopped sleeping well three quarters ago. Your team hit targets. You delivered the business case for the next round. But the thing driving it all is no longer ambition. It's just momentum dressed up as purpose.

## COMPRESSION v04
compression_family: C4
You manage people partially because you're good at it and partially because nobody else will carry the load the way you do. That second part is where burnout lives.
