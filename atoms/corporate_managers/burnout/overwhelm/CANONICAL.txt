path: burn rate
BAND: 3
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF5
---
There's a spreadsheet in your head. It's not written down, but it's very precise. Hours per person. Utilization rate. The point at which someone will start looking for another job. You know your team's numbers. Sarah is at eighty-five percent utilization and she has a side project. That's your worry. Marcus is fine at seventy-five but he's got aging parents. That's context you carry. Jackson's at ninety but he's junior and building skills. That's acceptable. You balance these equations constantly. It's not mathematical, exactly. It's intuition plus data plus the weight of knowing that if you miscalculate, someone quits, and then everyone's number goes up, and then it accelerates. You've seen it happen. You've been the person it happened to, years ago. Now you're trying to keep your team out of that spiral. But the numbers don't move. The deadlines don't move. So you just manage the burn and hope you're reading the smoke right.
