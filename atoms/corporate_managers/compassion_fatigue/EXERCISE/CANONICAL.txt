# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Voice: second-person imperative, somatic, guided
# Spec: 100–300 words, ≤10 words per instruction, explicit counts, no options, one step per sentence
# Persona grounding: desk, conference room, car, office, bathroom

---
## EXERCISE v01: The Emotional Boundary Setting
---
Before a hard conversation, pause. Say to yourself: their emotions are theirs. My emotions are mine. I can listen. I cannot absorb. Repeat this three times. Feel the boundary settle. You're opening your heart. You're also protecting your heart. Both are possible. Enter the conversation. You're present. You're separate.

---
## EXERCISE v02: The Worry Delegation Meditation
---
Sit at your desk. Name three worries about your team. Say each one aloud. Now say: this is not my responsibility to fix alone. I've done what I can. I release the rest. Feel the release. It's actually happening. Your shoulders are lower. Your chest is lighter. The worry isn't gone. You're not carrying it alone. You've shared the burden with reality. With time. With them.

---
## EXERCISE v03: The Empathy Overdose Recovery
---
You've absorbed too much today. Sit in your car. Close your eyes. Say: I am not them. I am me. Repeat five times slowly. Feel the separation between you and them. You're different people. You have different bodies. Different problems. Different futures. You can care and be separate. You can be compassionate and boundaried. The empathy overdose fades. You become yourself again.

---
## EXERCISE v04: The Container Release Practice
---
You've been the container. Your team's emotions are in your body. Stand up. Put both hands on your heart. Say: I release what is not mine. I release what I cannot carry. Breathe in. Breathe out. Breathe in. Breathe out. Do this five times. You're not releasing your compassion. You're releasing the burden of their problems. You're lighter. You're still compassionate. You're just less drowning.

---
## EXERCISE v05: The Separation Breathing Before One-On-Ones
---
Before a difficult one-on-one. Close your door. Breathe in for four counts. Say: I am here to help. I am not here to absorb. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat five times. You're clear on your role. You're helping without absorbing. You're boundaried. You're compassionate. The distinction is clear now. Enter the meeting. You're holding both.

---
## EXERCISE v06: The Energy Protection Visualization
---
Close your eyes. Visualize a shield around you. It's made of light. It's permeable. Your compassion passes through. Their problems don't. Visualize this for one minute. Open your eyes. You're carrying the shield. You're shielded and open. The shield protects you. It doesn't close you. This is possible. Remember the shield.

---
## EXERCISE v07: The Narrative Separation Work
---
Your team member is struggling. You're thinking: I should fix this. Stop. Write down: this is their story. This is not my story. I can support. I cannot live their story. Read this three times. Your clarity is back. Your role is clear. You're the guide. They're the traveler. You can guide without traveling their path.

---
## EXERCISE v08: The Feeling Audit Exercise
---
What are you feeling right now. Write it down. Now ask: is this mine. Is this theirs. Or is it shared. Some feelings are yours. Some are theirs. Some are the resonance between you. Name each one. Separate what's yours. Claim your feelings. Release theirs. This work takes five minutes. It saves you hours of confusion.

---
## EXERCISE v09: The Walking Release Between Meetings
---
After a hard meeting with your team, walk. Walk for five minutes minimum. Feel your feet on the ground. Feel your heartbeat. Feel yourself returning to yourself. Talk to yourself: I'm separate from them. I'm okay. They're okay. Keep walking. Let the movement separate you. By the time you arrive at the next meeting, you're back. You're refreshed. You're boundaried.

---
## EXERCISE v10: The Desk Posture Reset
---
You're slumped from absorbing everyone's stress. Sit up. Roll your shoulders back three times. Place both feet firmly on ground. Breathe in deeply. You're taking back your space. Your posture says: I am here. I am separate. I am strong. Feel this in your body. Your body knows the truth. You're not drowning. You're standing. Carry this posture.

---
## EXERCISE v11: The Problem Ownership Clarity
---
Write down a problem your team is facing. Now answer: whose problem is this ultimately. The answer is usually: theirs. Your job is to help them solve it. Not to solve it. Write this distinction clearly. This clarity changes everything. You're a helper, not a fixer. Helpers are sustainable. Fixers burn out. You're a helper.

---
## EXERCISE v12: The After-Work Release Ritual
---
Before you leave work, stand at your desk. Say: I leave their problems here. I take my rest with me. Take a different route home if possible. Let the physical change signal the internal change. You're not taking work home. You're taking care of yourself. Your family deserves you rested. You deserve rest. The ritual marks the separation.

---
## EXERCISE v13: The Compassion Without Rescue
---
Someone is struggling. You feel the urge to rescue them. Pause. Say: I can be compassionate without fixing this. I can listen. I cannot solve their problems. Write down what they actually need to do. Your job is to support their journey. Not to walk it for them. Your compassion is real. Your boundaries are also real.

---
## EXERCISE v14: The Emotional Contagion Awareness
---
Your team is anxious. You're becoming anxious. Notice this. Name it: I'm experiencing emotional contagion. Their anxiety is contagious. I can notice it without catching it. You've named it. The power shifts. You're no longer infected. You're observing. You're still compassionate. You're also separate.

---
## EXERCISE v15: The Responsibility Reality Check
---
Write down the problems your team is facing. Next to each, write: whose responsibility is this. Some are theirs. Some are the company's. Some are shared. Most are not yours alone. You're not responsible for everything. Seeing this on paper makes it real. You can support. You're not responsible for solving everything. Relief is possible.

---
## EXERCISE v16: The Self-Compassion Break
---
You're exhausted from absorbing everyone's emotion. Pause. Say: I'm doing the best I can. This is hard work. I deserve compassion too. Place your hand on your heart. Breathe in. Breathe out slowly. Do this five times. You're extending the compassion to yourself. You're not less worthy than the people you help. Your suffering matters too.

---
## EXERCISE v17: The Boundary Language Practice
---
Say these phrases aloud: I care about you. I cannot fix this for you. I believe in you. These phrases say you're compassionate. And boundaried. Practice saying them. Hear how they sound. Your voice can be both warm and clear. Say them five times each. Your new language is becoming natural.

---
## EXERCISE v18: The Team Problem Processing
---
Your team is spiraling. You feel the pull to spiral with them. Instead, step back. Write down the facts. What's actually happening. Separate from the emotion. The emotion is theirs. The facts are the facts. Your clarity helps more than your drowning. You're honoring them by staying clear. You're honoring yourself too.

---
## EXERCISE v19: The Energy Reserve Check
---
How full is your emotional tank right now. On a scale of one to ten. If it's below five, you must do something restorative before more serving. Schedule this. Don't feel guilty. You can't pour from an empty cup. Fill your cup. Then return to service. Your reserves matter. Protect them.

---
## EXERCISE v20: The Witnessing Without Fixing
---
Someone shares a problem. You listen completely. You say: I hear you. That's hard. What do you think you'll do. You've witnessed them. You haven't fixed them. Witnessing is powerful. Fixing isn't your job anyway. You're modeling that they're capable. You're not rescuing. You're honoring their agency.

---
## EXERCISE v21: The Feeling Stops Here Exercise
---
Put a line across your notebook. On top: the feelings I'm absorbing. Below: the feelings stop here. The feelings don't come home. The feelings don't stay in me. Visualize the line as real. Your notebook shows the truth. The feelings stop here. You're free.

---
## EXERCISE v22: The One-Person Company Reality
---
You're acting like you're responsible for everyone. You're one person. Say this aloud: I am one person. I have limits. This company has a structure. I'm one part. I'm not the whole. Other people have responsibilities too. Feel the relief. You're not carrying the company. You're carrying your part.

---
## EXERCISE v23: The Emotion Journaling
---
Every evening, write: what emotions did I absorb today. Write them. Now write: I release these emotions. I choose to keep my own. Write it three times. The journaling processes what you've absorbed. Writing releases it. Your notebook becomes the container. Your body is free.

---
## EXERCISE v24: The Compassion Audit
---
How much did you give today. On a scale of one to ten, what percentage was required. What percentage was extra. The extra is compassion fatigue. Noticing this helps you calibrate. You can give more when rested. You give less when tired. You're learning to read yourself.

---
## EXERCISE v25: The Tired Boundaries
---
When you're tired, your boundaries erode. Acknowledge this. When tired, you say yes more. When tired, you absorb more. That's not weakness. That's human. So when tired, protect yourself more. Your tired boundaries are tighter. Your tired heart is more vulnerable. Protect it more. Rest first. Serve second.

## EXERCISE v26: The Separation Ritual
---
End of workday. Touch your heart. Say: my heart is mine. I choose what enters it. I release what I absorbed. Say it three times. Feel the boundary. You're not closing your heart. You're just saying: I take care of my heart too. It's a healthy ritual.

---
## EXERCISE v27: The Honest Capacity Statement
---
Write: what I can actually handle. Write: what I'm being asked to handle. Look at both. They don't match. That's not failure. That's reality. Adjust one or the other. Tell people: my capacity is this. This isn't personal rejection. This is honest assessment. The honesty helps everyone.

## EXERCISE v28: The Gratitude for Your Heart
---
You have a compassionate heart. Thank it. Say: my heart, I'm grateful you care. I'm also protecting you now. You've been protecting me by caring. Now I protect you by boundaries. Your heart is safe. Your heart is strong. Thank your heart. Feel the gratitude. It's real.

## EXERCISE v29: The Shared Humanity
---
Your team is struggling. So are you. You're all humans struggling. Say this: we're all doing our best. We're all carrying things. My role is to be a human who cares. Not a superhero who fixes everything. Humans have limits. Humans need rest. I'm human. I have limits. This is okay.

## EXERCISE v30: The Sustainable Service Commitment
---
You commit to serving your team sustainably. That means: I'll take care of myself. I'll set boundaries. I'll rest when needed. I'll be present when I serve. This is better service. This is sustainable. Write this commitment. Sign it. This is your promise to yourself and your team.
