## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: ABSORPTION-RECOGNITION
---
The reorg announcement was Tuesday. By Wednesday, I was carrying seven different people's fears in my chest. Sarah's worry about her team being dissolved. Marcus's question about whether he'd made the cut. The collective anxiety in the all-hands chat during my Q&A. My job is to absorb this, translate it upward, soften it downward. My body does the math automatically: shoulders hunched, jaw tight, that hollow feeling in my stomach that comes from holding everyone else's panic while maintaining a calm exterior. The reframe isn't that I'm empathetic—it's that I'm porous. I'm letting their fear move through me without any protection. My chest is storing their anxiety like a hard drive. The weight lives here now.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: CARE-DEPLETION
---
I cared about this team. I still do. But the caring has become automatic now, like breathing—something I do without deciding. After the reorg, three people came to me in tears. I listened. I validated. I said things that were probably true and definitely hollow. My voice was steady. My hands were cold. The reframe is that I'm not running out of compassion because I'm a bad person or weak. I'm running out because I've been pouring it out with no intake. I've been the only container holding everyone's grief, and there's no one holding mine. My body is learning not to feel it anymore. That's not wisdom. That's numbness.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: EMOTIONAL-TRANSFER
---
I brought the team's panic home. I didn't talk about it—it was worse than that. It was silent. My partner asked what was wrong. I said nothing. What I meant was: everyone at work is terrified and I'm supposed to have the answer and I don't. My hands were jittery. I drank more wine than I planned. I checked email at 11 PM looking for some kind of resolution that wasn't there. The reframe is that I absorbed their emotional state and now it's mine. I'm not just empathizing anymore—I'm contaminated by it. My nervous system is synced to their dread. The boundary between their anxiety and my anxiety has completely dissolved.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: ROLE-CONFUSION
---
What am I supposed to do with this? That's the real question. I'm a manager, so I'm supposed to be the stable one, the translator, the person who helps people make sense of chaos. I'm supposed to be a container. But containers fill up. My container is full. I'm walking into meetings with my jaw already aching from holding tension, and then I open my mouth and my voice doesn't sound like me. It sounds like someone pretending everything is manageable. The reframe is that I've never learned the difference between being present for people and being responsible for their feelings. I'm carrying the weight as if it's mine to carry.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: FATIGUE-LOCATION
---
The fatigue isn't from working too much. It's from feeling too much. I can't even name it clearly because it lives in so many places at once: the heaviness behind my eyes, the way my chest doesn't expand all the way when I breathe, the dull ache in my neck. This is what compassion fatigue actually is—not running out of care, but the physical exhaustion of holding other people's pain while pretending it's not weighing on you. My body has started keeping score. A team member cries in my office and I can feel my nervous system shift: I'm now responsible for their recovery, their hope, their sense that someone senior understands. I'm the bucket they pour into.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: BOUNDARY-ABSENCE
---
There's no one above me feeling responsible for my anxiety about the reorg. I feel responsible for everyone below me. That asymmetry lives in my shoulders. My boss checks in: "How are you holding up?" I say fine. That word is armor. Below me, people reach out constantly—they need reassurance, clarity, to know I believe in them. I give it. I give everything. The reframe is that this isn't generosity. It's a power structure. I'm expected to absorb their fear because I'm paid more and have more security. But I'm still afraid. I'm just not allowed to say it.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: NUMBNESS-RECOGNITION
---
I noticed something dangerous last week: someone told me they were worried about layoffs and I felt nothing. Not cold, not indifferent—just empty. The pathway between their pain and my response has calcified. I said the right things. My face was appropriately serious. But something inside me just recorded it as data instead of feeling it. The reframe is that this is what happens when you absorb too much without releasing it. Your nervous system starts to shut down the input so it doesn't have to process the unbearable. I'm becoming numb to protect myself. The cost of that protection is that I'm no longer the person they think I am.
