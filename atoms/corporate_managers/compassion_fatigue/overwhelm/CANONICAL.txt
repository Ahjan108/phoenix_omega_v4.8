## STORY v01
---
path: the inbox that never empties
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
You have seven direct reports. Each one sends you approximately four emails a day about things that need your input. Decision. Approval. Clarification. Then there are the meetings where they ask the same things verbally because email might get lost. Then there are your peers' emails. Your director's messages. The monthly reviews that need writing, the feedback that needs integrating, the performance plans that need adjusting. You wake up to thirty emails. By noon you're at seventy. By end of day you're back to fifty because you've answered twenty and gotten thirty more while you were in back-to-back meetings. The inbox is a perpetual state of behind. You've stopped reading everything thoroughly. You're scanning. You're responding with shorter messages. You're saying "let's sync" which means another meeting. You're not keeping up with the actual content of people's work anymore. You're just managing the flow of information about work. Something is being lost in that transition.
