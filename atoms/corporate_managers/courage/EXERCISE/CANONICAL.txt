# corporate_managers × courage × difficult_action
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Voice: second-person imperative, somatic, guided
# Spec: 100–300 words, ≤10 words per instruction, explicit counts, no options, one step per sentence
# Persona grounding: desk, conference room, car, office, hallway

---
## EXERCISE v01: The Courage Warm-Up
---
Stand up from your desk. Place your feet firmly. Take three very deep breaths. On the third exhale, make a sound. A sigh. A groan. Anything. You're releasing fear. You're waking your body. You're preparing for courage. Sit back down. Your body is ready. Your mind is ready. Your courage is available. Use it.

---
## EXERCISE v02: The Script That Holds You
---
Write down exactly what you'll say. Write it word for word. Read it aloud five times. Hear your voice saying it. Your script is your anchor. You won't have to think. The words are already there. Your mind is freed. You can focus on breathing. On staying present. Your script is your safety net.

---
## EXERCISE v03: The Courage Visualization
---
Close your eyes. Visualize yourself saying the hard thing. See yourself calm. See yourself speaking clearly. See the conversation continuing. See yourself surviving it. Open your eyes. You've already done it mentally. Your nervous system knows it's possible. When it happens for real, you're remembering, not discovering.

---
## EXERCISE v04: The Body Preparation Practice
---
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Feel your ground. Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart is strong. It's designed for stress. It can handle this. Roll your shoulders back. Lift your chin slightly. You're physically preparing for courage. Your body is ready. Your mind follows your body.

---
## EXERCISE v05: The Voice Strength Exercise
---
Say your difficult truth in a whisper. Say it again, louder. Say it again, full voice. Your voice is getting stronger. Your conviction is growing. By the third time, you own it. Your voice carries your courage. Use this same progression in the real conversation. Start soft. Build to strength.

---
## EXERCISE v06: The Fear Naming Exercise
---
Name exactly what you're afraid will happen. Write it down. Now ask: what's the actual probability of this. Usually it's low. Very low. Write the realistic probability. You're replacing catastrophic thinking with reality. Your fear shrinks. Your courage grows. You're no longer fighting imaginary disasters. You're facing reality. Reality is manageable.

---
## EXERCISE v07: The Five-Minute Commitment
---
Commit to five minutes of courage. Not forever. Not the whole conversation. Five minutes. You can do anything for five minutes. This removes the overwhelm. You're not committing to hours. You're committing to five minutes. The rest will follow.

---
## EXERCISE v08: The Worst-Case Survival Plan
---
What's the worst that could happen. Write it. Now ask: could I survive this. The answer is always: yes. Could it be uncomfortable. Yes. Could I survive it. Always yes. You're separating discomfort from death. Courage is doing hard things even though they're uncomfortable. You'll survive. That's all you need to know.

---
## EXERCISE v09: The Grounding Before the Moment
---
Right before the hard conversation. Stop. Feel your feet. Say: I am here. I am safe. I am ready. Repeat three times. Your nervous system settles. Your clarity returns. You're not in fight-or-flight. You're in presence. This presence is courage. Walk in with it.

---
## EXERCISE v10: The Breath That Holds You
---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Do this three times before you need courage. Your nervous system recognizes this breath. When you use it during the hard moment, you're already calm. The breath is your anchor. Use it.

---
## EXERCISE v11: The Ally Recall
---
Before you need courage, call to mind someone you admire. Someone who's brave. What would they do. What would they say. Now become that person. Not pretending. Channeling. You have their strength available. Use it. You're not alone. You're drawing from the strength of people you admire.

---
## EXERCISE v12: The Values Alignment Check
---
Is this action aligned with your values. Answer yes or no. If yes, you have your reason. I'm doing this because it's right. Not because it's easy. Not because I'm comfortable. Because it's right. That reason is stronger than fear. Hold that reason. It's your courage.

---
## EXERCISE v13: The Small Action First
---
You need courage for a big thing. Start small. Text the person. That's courage. Next day, send the email. That's more. Next week, have the conversation. You're building courage momentum. Small actions build to big actions. Start tiny. Build from there.

---
## EXERCISE v14: The Mirror Work
---
Stand in front of a mirror. Make eye contact with yourself. Say the hard thing. Watch yourself say it. Your reflection is facing you with honesty. That reflection can do this. You can do this. Make eye contact. Hold the gaze. You're brave. Your reflection knows it.

---
## EXERCISE v15: The Success Anchor
---
Remember a time you did something hard. You were scared. You did it anyway. You survived. You're okay. You're even proud. Remember that feeling. Feel it in your body now. That person who did that hard thing. That person is you. You've done hard things. You can do this one.

---
## EXERCISE v16: The Permission You Need
---
Give yourself permission. Say aloud: I give myself permission to be afraid. I give myself permission to speak anyway. I give myself permission to mess up. I give myself permission to try. You're not waiting for permission from anyone else. You're giving it to yourself. That's all you need.

---
## EXERCISE v17: The Physical Courage Practice
---
Do something small that scares you today. Make a call you've been avoiding. Send an email you've been delaying. Ask a question you've been holding. These are small courage moments. They're preparing you for the big one. Courage is a muscle. Use it. It grows.

---
## EXERCISE v18: The Uncomfortable Comfort
---
Discomfort is not danger. Say this three times. Feel the discomfort in your body. Now ask: am I in actual danger. The answer is: no. I'm just uncomfortable. Discomfort is survivable. Discomfort is what courage feels like. Discomfort and courage are the same. Lean into the discomfort. You're leaning into courage.

---
## EXERCISE v19: The Witness Confidence
---
Who do you want witnessing your courage. Imagine them. A mentor. A friend. A therapist. Someone who believes in you. They're in the room with you mentally. Feel their belief. You're not alone. They're rooting for you. Their belief in you matters. Use it.

---
## EXERCISE v20: The After Celebration Plan
---
Plan how you'll celebrate after you're brave. What will you do. Where will you go. How will you mark that you did it. Having this plan makes the courage seem worth it. You're not just being brave for hard reasons. You're being brave and then celebrating. Plan the celebration.

---
## EXERCISE v21: The Fear Naming In Real Time
---
In the moment of courage. If fear arrives. Name it. Say: I'm afraid. That's okay. I'm continuing anyway. You're not pretending to not be afraid. You're afraid and moving forward. That's courage. That's what courage actually is. Afraid and moving. You're doing it.

---
## EXERCISE v22: The Courage Journal
---
Write down three times you've been brave. Write what made you brave. Write what happened after. Read these. You're a person with a history of courage. This isn't your first time. You know how to do this. Remember your history. Write it. Feel it. It's real.

---
## EXERCISE v23: The Value of Discomfort
---
Write down: what will this discomfort lead to. Usually: growth. Change. Authenticity. Relief. These are valuable. The discomfort is the price of the value. You're buying something important. The discomfort is the currency. You can afford it. You can spend it. The value is worth it.

---
## EXERCISE v24: The Body Scanning for Courage
---
Where is your courage in your body. Is it in your chest. Your belly. Your legs. Locate it. Put your hand there. Feel it. It's real. It's physical. You're not making it up. Your body knows how to be brave. Your body is brave. Let your body lead.

---
## EXERCISE v25: The Words That Empower
---
Create a power phrase. I am brave. I speak truth. I show up. Whatever resonates. Say it five times. Feel the power. Use it right before you need courage. Your phrase is your reset. Your phrase is your reminder. I am this. I can do this.

---
## EXERCISE v26: The Breath of the Brave
---
Imagine a person you think is brave. How do they breathe. Probably calm. Probably steady. Breathe like them. Your breath becomes theirs. Your nervous system recognizes the pattern. You're brave. You're breathing like the brave. You're becoming brave. Breathe.

---
## EXERCISE v27: The Courage Momentum
---
You did one brave thing. Do another. Small one. Then another. You're building momentum. Courage feeds courage. Action feeds action. Each brave thing makes the next one easier. You're not trying to jump from zero to hero. You're building one step at a time. You're doing it.

---
## EXERCISE v28: The Release After Courage
---
You did the brave thing. It's over. Now release. Shake your body. Make noise. Move around. You're releasing the adrenaline. You're completing the nervous system cycle. You did it. You're okay. You can breathe now. You can rest now. You did it. Release it.

---
## EXERCISE v29: The Courage Gratitude
---
Thank yourself. Say: I'm grateful I was brave. I'm grateful I spoke up. I'm grateful I tried. You're reinforcing the courage. You're making it a positive memory. You're building identity: I am someone who is brave. This identity compounds. You become braver.

---
## EXERCISE v30: The Never-Alone Knowing
---
Millions of people have done hard things. They were scared. They did it anyway. You're part of that group now. You're never alone. The human experience includes courage. You're human. You're included. You're part of the courageous humans. Feel that. Know that. Use that.
