# corporate_managers × depression × integration_landing
# Slot type: INTEGRATION
# Count: 7
# Voice: first-person author, quiet, concrete
# Spec: 120-200 words, 1 reframe, 1 carry line ≤8 words, no reassurance, concrete body state

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: PROMOTION-PARADOX
---
I got the promotion and the flatness came with it. This was supposed to fix something. I was supposed to feel different, bigger, more myself. Instead I wake up and there's a heaviness across my chest that doesn't lift. My leg bounces in meetings. I can't focus on email without reading the same sentence four times. The reframe isn't that promotions don't matter—it's that they don't matter to whatever is actually broken in me. I move into a bigger office and the depression moves with me. It takes up space in the new title, the new salary, the new weight. I'm supposed to feel successful. I feel numb. The body keeps score.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: ISOLATION-BY-ROLE
---
I can't talk to my team about how empty this feels. I can't tell them that I got the thing I was supposed to want and it doesn't feel like anything. There's no one at my level I can be honest with. My peer in the other division seems fine—we text occasionally about targets and strategy, but I can't text "I feel like nothing matters anymore." My wife asks why I'm quiet and I say work is intense. What I mean is: I'm leading people and I don't believe in myself anymore. The reframe is that leadership is isolating even when you're surrounded by people. Maybe especially then. My silence is the required posture. My hands know this. There's no one to reach out to.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: EXPECTATION-MISMATCH
---
I thought the role would change me. I'd be at a different table now. The decisions would matter more. I'd feel less like an imposter. None of that happened. I'm in meetings with the senior leadership and I feel more like an imposter than I did as an IC. I can fake presence. My voice can sound authoritative. But inside I'm calculating how long until they realize I'm not qualified for this. The reframe is that the promotion didn't fix the fundamental doubt—it amplified it. I'm more visible now. More eyes on my decisions. The depression grew because the spotlight grew. I feel smaller in a bigger role.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MEANING-ABSENCE
---
What is this for? That's the question that wakes me up at 3 AM now. I'm making good money. I have direct reports. I'm building something. But there's a baseline flatness underneath everything. Nothing feels like it matters enough to get excited about. My team gives presentations and I'm supposed to feel pride. I feel tired. The reframe is that depression isn't sadness—it's the absence of meaning-making. My brain has stopped generating the stories that make this feel like it matters. I can see the logic: you've accomplished the thing, you should feel satisfied. My body isn't convinced. My shoulders stay hunched.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: AUTONOMIC-SHUTDOWN
---
My body has gone quiet in a way that's almost worse than anxiety. No adrenaline. No spike of fear. Just a persistent low-level shutdown. I wake up tired. Shower tired. Get through calls tired. The coffee doesn't help. The sun doesn't help. Evening comes and I've accomplished nothing that felt alive. The reframe is that this particular depression is my nervous system giving up on the story. It tried hope. It tried achievement. It tried believing in the path forward. It's stopped trying. My chest doesn't expand properly when I breathe. My hands are cold. The physical flatness is the depression—the thoughts follow after.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: PERFORMANCE-COST
---
They can't know. That's the governing rule now. I show up on calls with an executive presence I had to practice. I facilitate meetings where I'm supposed to have perspective. I mentor people who are looking to me for direction, and I feel like I'm faking everything. The reframe is that performing competence while experiencing depression uses a specific kind of energy—the energy of self-betrayal. I'm disconnecting from myself in real-time every single day. The cost accumulates. My face feels tense. My jaw is permanently clenched. I'm living two lives: the visible one and the one where nothing seems worth doing.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: SUCCESS-FAILURE
---
I did everything right and ended up here anyway. The promotion was the reward for doing things correctly. And now I'm in a role that's supposed to feel like the culmination, and I just feel distant from everything. My team is engaged. The work is important. The compensation is what I'd hoped for. But there's a persistent thought underneath: none of this fixes the fundamental fact that I don't believe in myself. The reframe is that success doesn't cure doubt. Sometimes it confirms it. I'm at the top of a ladder I never wanted to climb. My legs know this. There's no joy at this height.
