

## EXERCISE v01: The Naming The Loss
---

---
Say aloud what you've lost. The person. The team. The culture. Say it. Naming makes it real. Real is the only path through. Name your loss. Say it multiple times. Hear yourself say it. The loss is real. You're acknowledging it. That matters.
---

## EXERCISE v02: The Grief Written
---

---
Write a letter to what you've lost. Say everything. Don't send it. Write for ten minutes minimum. Let the words come. You're expressing the grief. Writing releases it. Don't edit. Just write. When done, fold it. The grief is contained. That's enough.
---

## EXERCISE v03: The Grief Timeline
---

---
When did you start missing them. Mark it on a calendar. When did it get worse. Mark that. When did it start getting easier. Mark that. You're visualizing your grief. Visualizing grief helps you move through it. You're not stuck. You're moving.
---

## EXERCISE v04: The Loss Inventory
---

---
What did they bring to your life. List everything. Their humor. Their wisdom. Their presence. Their friendship. List it. Now list what you're learning without them. How you're adapting. How you're growing. You're seeing both. Both are real.
---

## EXERCISE v05: The Memory Savoring
---

---
Pick one memory. Let yourself feel it fully. Remember their laugh. Remember their words. Remember the moment. Feel the sadness. Feel the joy. Feel both together. This is how love survives loss. You hold both. That's grief. That's also love.
---

## EXERCISE v06: The Ritual For Goodbye
---

---
Create a small goodbye ritual. Light a candle. Write something. Read something. Do something that marks: I'm saying goodbye. The ritual closes a door. The door closing lets you move forward. Create your ritual. Do it. Say goodbye.
---

## EXERCISE v07: The Feelings Sit With
---

---
Sit with one feeling for five minutes. Sadness. Anger. Relief. Whatever comes. Don't try to change it. Just sit with it. Feel it in your body. Let it be. Feelings aren't permanent. Sitting with them lets them move. Sit. Feel. Let it move.
---

## EXERCISE v08: The Photo Work
---

---
Look at a photo of what you've lost. Let yourself cry. Let yourself smile. Let yourself feel. Your eyes on their image. Your heart in your chest. The grief is love. The tears are love. Let them come.
---

## EXERCISE v09: The Gratitude For The Time
---

---
Write three things you're grateful for from what you lost. The time you had. The things you learned. The impact they had. Gratitude doesn't erase the loss. It honors the gift. Write three. Feel the honor.
---

## EXERCISE v10: The Body Holds The Grief
---

---
Where do you feel the grief in your body. Your chest. Your throat. Your belly. Your heart. Put your hand there. Feel the grief physically. You're acknowledging that your body grieves. Your body knows the truth. Your body is wise. Listen to it.
---

## EXERCISE v11: The Letter You Can't Send
---

---
Write the letter you wish you'd sent. Before they left. Before the change. What would you say. Say it now. In writing. To no one but yourself. You're expressing what's inside. You're releasing it. Write. Say what you needed to say.
---

## EXERCISE v12: The Grief Walk
---

---
Walk to a place that reminds you of what you've lost. Walk slowly. Feel your feet. Let the memory come. Let the sadness come. Walk. You're honoring the loss by moving your body. Movement processes grief. Walk.
---

## EXERCISE v13: The Energy Shift Journal
---

---
When do you feel the loss most. Morning. Afternoon. Evening. Write when. Then write: what helps during this time. Being alone. Being with people. Moving. Resting. You're building your grief toolkit. You're learning what helps. Journal this.
---

## EXERCISE v14: The Acceptance Affirmation
---

---
Say: This loss is real. This pain is real. I'm allowing myself to grieve. Say it three times daily. Your mind starts accepting. Your body starts processing. Acceptance is not okay-ness. It's acknowledgment. Affirm the acknowledgment.
---

## EXERCISE v15: The Small Task As Tribute
---

---
Do something small in their honor. Donate. Volunteer. Write. Speak. Create something. This action honors them. Your action acknowledges them. You're keeping them part of your story. Small action. Big meaning.
---

## EXERCISE v16: The Story You're Telling
---

---
How are you talking about what you've lost. If negative, reframe. Include: what I learned. How I'm growing. How they impacted me. The story includes the loss. And your resilience. Tell yourself the full story.
---

## EXERCISE v17: The Feelings Fluctuation Acceptance
---

---
Some days you'll feel fine. Some days the loss hits. Both are normal. You're not failing. You're grieving. Grief isn't linear. The waves come. The waves pass. Let them come. Let them pass. Don't judge yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v18: The Conversation You'd Have
---

---
Imagine them here. What would you say. Say it aloud. One-sided conversation. Your words matter. You're still connected. The connection didn't end. It transformed. Have the conversation. Say what you need to say.
---

## EXERCISE v19: The Integration Moment
---

---
One day you'll realize: I'm thinking about them without pain. Maybe happiness. Maybe bittersweet. But not crushing. When that happens, notice it. Honor it. That's integration. That's the grief transforming. You're moving through. That moment will come.
---

## EXERCISE v20: The Grief Witnesses
---

---
Tell one person how much you're grieving. Really tell them. Not polite version. Real version. Their witness matters. Grief needs witnessing. Grief expressed to another becomes less isolating. Tell one person. Be real.
---

## EXERCISE v21: The Photo Album
---

---
Go through photos. Old ones. From when they were here. Let yourself feel. Laugh. Cry. Feel whatever comes. You're honoring the past. You're acknowledging it was real. Photos are proof. Review them. Honor them.
---

## EXERCISE v22: The Letter From Them
---

---
Write a letter from them to you. What would they say. What would they want you to know. Write it. Let the love and wisdom flow. You're accessing what they taught you. You're keeping them present. Write.
---

## EXERCISE v23: The Grief Permission
---

---
Give yourself permission to grieve. Not for a set time. But for as long as you need. Grief doesn't have a deadline. You're allowed to miss them. You're allowed to feel it. Give yourself permission. Say it aloud.
---

## EXERCISE v24: The New Normal Acceptance
---

---
The new normal is not bad. It's just different. They're not here. The structure is different. The energy is different. It's not wrong. It's just different. Accept the different. Grieve the change. Move into the different. It's your life now.
---

## EXERCISE v25: The Impact They Had
---

---
Write how they impacted you. How you're different because of them. How you're stronger. How you're wiser. How they're still in your decisions. They're still here. Their impact is real. Write the impact. You're honoring them.
---

## EXERCISE v26: The Grief Buddy
---

---
Find someone else who's grieving. Talk about it. Shared grief is less isolating. You're not alone. Others know this feeling. Connect with them. Grief shared is grief that lightens. Find your buddy. Connect.
---

## EXERCISE v27: The Forward Movement
---

---
You're not forgetting them. You're moving forward with them. Like they're still in your backpack. A weight. Also a comfort. You move and they come with you. That's integration. That's love after loss. You're doing this.
---

## EXERCISE v28: The Grief Meditation
---

---
Close your eyes. Feel your breath. Feel your heartbeat. Feel the loss in your body. Now feel: you're still here. Your heart still beats. Your breath still comes. The loss is real. You're also still here. Both. You're holding both.
---

## EXERCISE v29: The Ritual Return
---

---
Once yearly, return to your goodbye ritual. Remember them. Honor them. Let the grief come. Then let it go. Yearly marking of the loss. This keeps the relationship alive. This honors what was. Do this yearly. It helps.
---

## EXERCISE v30: The Love Remains
---

---
The loss is huge. The love is bigger. Say this. Feel this. The love doesn't go away. The person does. The love stays. You're grieving because you loved. The grief is proof of the love. The love was real. The love is still real.
---
