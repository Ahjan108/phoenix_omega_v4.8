## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: B

What I notice is a particular kind of numbness that emerges after prolonged emotional labor. A student shares something difficult and I respond appropriately. I listen. I validate. I hold space. But there is something absent in my own internal experience. I am going through the motions of compassion while experiencing a kind of emptiness beneath it. The mechanism I recognize is what I think of as compassion-depletion. The system that generates empathetic response is like a well that can be drawn from, and the well has a finite depth. When the drawing continues long past the replenishment, the well begins to run dry. What emerges is a state where I am still showing up, still responding, still holding space, but the actual feeling is not there. What I have come to understand is that this is not moral failure. This is not evidence that I do not care. This is the nervous system responding to a demand that exceeds its capacity to regenerate the response. The mechanism of compassion fatigue is not a character flaw.

I notice the particular quality of decision-making that happens when I am fatigued. A student who would normally receive my careful attention becomes someone I manage more efficiently. A parent concern that would normally receive a thoughtful response becomes something I minimize so I can move on. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as fatigue-driven-triage. The mechanism operates below conscious decision-making. I do not consciously decide to care less about certain students. But the depletion creates a kind of automatic triage where my limited attention is allocated to the most pressing matters. And the definition of pressing changes when I am depleted. What I have come to understand is that this mechanism is partially adaptive. In a situation with finite resources and infinite needs, some form of triage is necessary. But the mechanism does not distinguish between rational triage and the triage that comes from fatigue. The fatigue-driven triage is not based on actual importance. It is based on what feels easiest to manage in my depleted state.
