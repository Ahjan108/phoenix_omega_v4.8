## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
Courage in the classroom is the choice made while you are afraid the lesson will fail, while you are afraid the student will reject you, while you are afraid you are not enough. It is the move you make while your hands are shaking. The system works like this: you feel the full weight of the risk. You do not dismiss it. And then you teach anyway. This is what happens when something matters more than the fear.

---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
The cost of not being courageous is sometimes greater than the cost of being courageous. You know this. You know that staying small, staying in the safe lesson, staying inside the boundary of what is already known, costs something. It costs the student you might have reached. It costs the potential that might have emerged. The refusal to move is not safe. It is just a different kind of risk.

---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
Your body knows the difference between fear that is stopping you and fear that is informing you. When you listen to the fear but do not obey it, your chest expands slightly. Your breath steadies. You are no longer fighting yourself. You are no longer in conflict. The physical sensation is of alignment. You are scared and also moving. That is what courage feels like in the body.

## COMPRESSION v04
---
compression_family: C4
---
The relational consequence of acting courageously is that you give permission to students to act courageously too. You do not have to talk about it. Your actions teach what is possible. When they see you make the vulnerable ask, they know they can too. When they see you fail and continue, they know failure is survivable. You become proof. This is how students learn courage. Not from lectures. From watching you do it.

## COMPRESSION v05
---
compression_family: C5
---
The temporal rhythm of courage is that it gets easier the more times you do it. Your nervous system learns that the scary thing does not kill you. It learns the pattern. The next time you face something similar, the activation is lower. The recovery is faster. Courage is not a one-time event. It is a skill that improves with repetition. Over time, you are no longer thinking about being brave. You are just being.

## COMPRESSION v06
---
compression_family: C1
---
What courage does is make the threat real but still choose to move. It is a specific kind of clarity. You see exactly what could go wrong and you teach anyway because something else is also true: the student needs you to try. The system is not "feel brave." The system is "acknowledge the risk and move toward what the student needs." That is available to you right now.

## COMPRESSION v07
---
compression_family: C2
---
The cost of not moving is a slow calcification. You stay in the same lesson plans. The same approaches. The same safety. You know something different might work but you do not try. You know a student might respond to a different approach but you do not risk it. Over time, teaching becomes rote. You have become a delivery system instead of an educator. The student loses. The teacher loses. The cost is measured in potential unrealized.

## COMPRESSION v08
---
compression_family: C3
---
Your nervous system is designed to move toward what is important. When you do not move, even when you want to, it creates internal friction. Your body registers the gap between what you want to do and what you are doing. The stress is not from the courageous action. It is from the refusal to act. Moving courageously, even with fear, reduces the internal conflict. The body wants to move.

If you do not take risks when they need you to, you teach them that it is not safe to take risks. If you do take risks, you teach them that meaningful things require courage. This is who you become through action.

## COMPRESSION v10
---
compression_family: C5
---
The pattern is instructive. The first time you tried something new, you were terrified. The second time was still scary but less paralyzing. The third time, the fear had a familiar shape. By the twentieth time, you were just doing it even though the fear was there. You did not become fearless. You became practiced at moving while scared. The fear never left. Your response to the fear changed. That is courage.

## COMPRESSION v11
---
compression_family: C1
---
The mechanism is simple: you gather information about the risk, you calculate your capacity to handle it, and then you decide whether the student's growth is worth the risk. If it is, you move. This is not recklessness. This is informed choice. You are not ignoring the fear. You are considering it and deciding that the thing you want is still more important than the thing you fear. That is the whole system.
