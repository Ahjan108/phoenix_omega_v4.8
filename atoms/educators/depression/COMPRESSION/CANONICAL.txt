## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
The mechanism of depression in teachers is that it arrives disguised as realism. You are not sad. You are seeing clearly that the system is broken and you cannot fix it. You are not unmotivated. You are recognizing that your effort does not actually matter. This is the trap. This is how depression sells itself to intelligent people. It comes as clarity instead of illness. By the time you realize something is wrong, you have adopted its logic completely.

---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
The cost of the relentless giving is that you arrive at the end of the day and feel nothing. The wins feel hollow. The connections feel empty. What should feel like the purpose of teaching feels like a con you have been running on yourself. The students did not learn what they should have learned and the cost is measured in their futures. The cost is also measured in your own collapse.

---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
Your body does not lie. When depression arrives, it comes as a heaviness that sleep does not touch. Your movements slow. Your voice lowers. The effort required to do the simplest thing increases. This is not laziness. This is what depression does to the nervous system. It dampens the signal. It makes everything require more energy. Your body is giving you information. It is telling you that something is fundamentally wrong.

---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
compression_family: C4
---
The relational consequence is that you have become unreachable to the students who need you. They cannot land on you with their questions. You cannot receive their enthusiasm. You cannot be present with their struggles. They interpret this as rejection. Actually, it is a symptom. You cannot let them in because there is no "in" anymore. The capacity to connect has been switched off. This isolation is not chosen. It is the structure of the depression.

## COMPRESSION v05
---
compression_family: C5
---
The pattern of depression does not announce itself with drama. It arrives gradually, like a room slowly losing light. First you do not notice it is darker. Then you do not notice that you are moving more slowly. Then you do not notice that you have stopped moving at all. By the time the darkness is complete, it feels like the natural state of things. You have forgotten that light is possible.

The system that held that teacher in place has been destabilized.
