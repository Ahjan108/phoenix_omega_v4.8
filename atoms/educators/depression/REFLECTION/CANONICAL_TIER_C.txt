What I notice is a particular quality of absence in the presence. I am teaching the lesson. I am managing the classroom. I am responding to students. But something fundamental is not there. The enthusiasm that used to animate the work has become absent. The sense of purpose that used to drive the work has become distant. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as presence-without-aliveness. The mechanism is the capacity to show up and do the work while experiencing a kind of internal deadness. The work happens. The output is there. But the inner experience is flat. The work is happening but I am not particularly there for it.
