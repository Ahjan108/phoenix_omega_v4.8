## EXERCISE v01
Sit at your desk. Close the laptop.
Place your hand on your heart.
Feel your heartbeat under your hand for one minute.
This is the heart that's been giving and giving.
Now take five deep breaths. Really deep ones.
On the exhale, say: "I receive from myself first."
Say it five times. Hear your voice say it.
You cannot pour from an empty cup.
Your cup has been empty for weeks. You know this.
Open a journal and write what you need right now.
Not what your clients need. Not what your team needs.
What do YOU need? Write three things.
Pick one of those three things.
Do that thing this week, even if it's small.
Even if it's a walk alone. Even if it's silence.

## EXERCISE v02
Write down every person you're supporting right now.
List them. All of them. Your clients, your team, your family.
How many names are on the list?
Now, write your own name at the top.
Your name should be the first one.
Draw a line through every name except yours.
Just for one day, practice this.
One day where you put yourself first.
Not as selfish. As necessary. As survival.
One day where you don't check emails.
One day where you don't take calls.
One day where you're not available to anyone.
The world will spin. Your clients will survive.
By the end of the day, you'll feel guilty.
This guilt is the cost of your habit.
Pay it. You've paid much higher costs.

## EXERCISE v03
You're in your coffee shop. Order something for yourself.
Not because you're hungry. Because you deserve it.
Sit down. Look at what you ordered.
Eat it slowly. Don't think about problems.
Think about the taste. Think about the warmth.
This is an act of self-compassion.
You're not used to receiving your own compassion.
You're used to giving it to everyone else.
Today, you're reversing the flow.
After you finish, text someone. One person.
Tell them one thing that's good about you.
Not in a bragging way. In a factual way.
"I'm good at listening." "I'm reliable." "I show up."
Speaking your own goodness is radical resistance.
Radical resistance to the voice that says you're never enough.

## EXERCISE v04
Pull out a timer. Set it for twenty minutes.
For twenty minutes, do absolutely nothing.
No phone. No laptop. No reading. No productivity.
Just sit. This is not meditation. It's surrender.
When the voice starts telling you what you should do, say: "Not now."
When guilt arrives, say: "I'll deal with you later."
When the to-do list starts running, say: "Quiet."
For twenty minutes, you're not available to responsibility.
You're available to yourself. That's it.
By minute fifteen, you'll be anxious.
By minute twenty, you'll feel something like relief.
This twenty minutes is a taste of what peace feels like.
You deserve to feel this regularly.
Next week, do this twice.
By month end, you'll have twelve hours of mercy.

## EXERCISE v05
Write down everything you've been giving away.
Your time. Your energy. Your emotional labor.
Your availability. Your sanity. Your peace.
Look at the list. Feel how long it is.
Now, circle three things you're ready to stop giving.
Maybe you stop being on call 24/7.
Maybe you stop giving free consulting to friends.
Maybe you stop solving everyone's emergencies.
Pick three. Write them down separately.
For each one, write: "I am taking this back."
Say it out loud three times. Believe it a little.
This week, implement one of the three.
Tell the relevant person: "I can't be available for this anymore."
They'll be disappointed. That's okay.
You've been disappointed by your own sacrifice. It's their turn to adapt.

## EXERCISE v06
Open your calendar. Find one hour this week.
Block it off. Label it: "Rest."
Put a calendar invite for yourself.
You're scheduling rest like you schedule meetings.
Because rest is as important as work.
During this hour, don't work. Don't email. Don't solve.
Go for a walk. Take a bath. Sleep.
Do nothing. Do the thing that feels most selfish.
That selfish thing is actually necessary.
Next week, block off two hours.
By next month, you'll have one full day.
One full day where you're not solving for anyone.
One full day where you're receiving instead of giving.
This day will save your life.
You'll know you're getting better when a full day feels possible.

## EXERCISE v07
Write down the three hardest emotions you avoid.
Maybe it's anger. Maybe it's sadness.
Maybe it's the realization that you're not okay.
Pick one. Sit with it for ten minutes.
Let yourself feel it. Don't fix it. Don't explain it.
Just feel it in your body.
Where do you feel it? In your chest? Your stomach?
Put your hand there and breathe into it.
You've been running from this feeling.
The feeling doesn't go away from running.
The feeling only goes away from feeling.
After ten minutes, you can move on.
But something will have shifted.
The monster under the bed is smaller when you look at it.
Do this once a week. The emotion will lose its power.

## EXERCISE v08
Create a rule: No working on anything after 8 PM.
Not emails. Not planning. Not thinking about work.
After 8 PM, you're a human, not a founder.
Tell your team this rule exists.
Tell your clients this rule exists.
Then enforce it with yourself most of all.
When the work impulse comes at 9 PM, notice it.
Say: "Tomorrow." Then go do something else.
This creates a boundary between you and your role.
The role is endless. You are not.
You'll feel like things aren't getting done.
Things are getting done. Just not at midnight.
By week three, you'll notice you're sleeping better.
By week four, you'll notice you're thinking clearer.
By week five, you'll notice you've gotten your mind back.
Your mind is your most valuable resource.
Stop giving it away after 8 PM.

## EXERCISE v09
Write yourself a letter from your own future self.
The future self is someone who got better.
Write: "I'm so glad you started taking care of yourself."
Write: "That rest you took changed everything."
Write three things that future-you is grateful for.
Seal the letter. Put it away.
Read it once a week for one month.
You're training your mind to believe in recovery.
Recovery is possible. That's the message.
You don't have to stay broken forever.
You don't have to keep giving until you're empty.
You're allowed to refill your own cup first.
After one month of reading the letter, write a new one.
This one is from today-you to tomorrow-you.
Say what you need. Say what you're committing to.
Mail it to yourself. Receive it as a letter.
You're worth receiving care from yourself.

## EXERCISE v10
Call someone you love. Not for them. For you.
Tell them you've been giving too much away.
Tell them you're trying to stop.
Ask them to help you remember your own needs.
This person can't solve your problem.
But they can witness you trying to solve it.
Witnessing is powerful.
Knowing someone sees you trying is powerful.
By the end of the conversation, you'll feel less alone.
The compassion fatigue is partly about isolation.
You're carrying everyone's problems alone.
Having someone know you're carrying is part of healing.
Call this person once a week.
They don't have to do anything.
Just listen while you remember you matter too.

Turn your phone completely off. Not silent. Off.
Set a timer for exactly five minutes.
Close your eyes and breathe slowly.

Hold for four counts.
Count to four on the exhale.
Repeat this cycle for the full five minutes.

Notice that nothing terrible happened in five minutes.
Your work is still there. The crisis is not.
Turn your phone back on.
You are now five minutes less anxious.

## EXERCISE v16
---

Set a phone reminder for 3 PM and 5 PM.
Between now and 3 PM, don't check the folder.
This creates a boundary.
The boundary protects your morning focus.
You're not ignoring problems. You're batching them.
Batching problems is more effective than scattering anxiety.
At 3 PM, open the folder and respond.
You've created a system of protection.
Systems are more reliable than willpower.
Work on your system, not just your willpower.
This system works because it removes the constant decision.
The constant decision was exhausting you.
The system lets you rest.
