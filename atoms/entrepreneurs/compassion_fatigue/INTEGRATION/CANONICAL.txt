## INTEGRATION v01
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT

I stopped taking my therapist's calls the minute they were scheduled. I started listening at 1.5x speed, absorbing but distant.

My chest was tight. My voice was small. I was empty but still showing up.

Then one day I couldn't. I canceled a client call to sleep. Just sleep.

The guilt was immediate. But underneath the guilt was relief. Profound, physical relief.

My body had finally refused. My mind had been willing to keep going forever.

Now I listen to what my body refuses before I have to refuse it for me.

**Carry line: My refusal is my body's wisdom.**

I'm learning to hear it.

## INTEGRATION v02
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: REORIENTATION

I realized I was holding everyone's problems like they were mine to solve.

A client was failing. I took it as my failure.

A team member was struggling. I made it my responsibility to fix them.

The weight of everyone's life was on my shoulders. And I'd volunteered for every ounce.

Now I ask: Is this my problem to solve?

Usually the answer is no.

When the answer is yes, I ask: Can I solve this in a way that doesn't require me to be superhuman?

This shifts everything. I can help my team. But not by absorbing their struggle. By holding boundaries and teaching them to handle it.

**Carry line: Helping doesn't mean saving.**

I'm helping by not saving.

## INTEGRATION v03
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: PATTERN-BREAK

I stopped being available 24/7. Not because I wanted to. Because I had to.

My doctor said: Your cortisol is dangerously high. Stop.

Stop what? Everything. Everything was the answer.

I told my team: I'm not available nights and weekends.

I told my clients: I work Monday through Friday, 9 to 5.

I told myself: This is not selfish.

The first month was panicky. The second month, calmer. By month three, I realized I hadn't missed anything critical.

The critical things weren't that critical. They just felt critical because I was running on adrenaline.

**Carry line: Availability was the sickness.**

Protection is the cure.

## INTEGRATION v04
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-CONSEQUENCE

When I'm giving everything away, my hands hurt. My head aches. I get sick constantly.

My body is speaking. It's saying: This is unsustainable.

I started tracking it. On weeks when I say no to extra requests, my body feels better.

On weeks when I say yes to everything, I'm in pain.

This is the data my body is giving me.

I'm finally listening.

**Carry line: The body knows what the mind denies.**

I'm trusting my body more.

## INTEGRATION v05
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: PROOF-COLLECTION

I started doing something small for myself every day.

A walk alone. A bath. A book. Something.

I tracked how I felt in weeks when I did this versus weeks when I didn't.

The difference was massive.

Self-care isn't indulgence. It's maintenance. It's what allows me to keep showing up.

Without maintenance, the machine breaks.

**Carry line: Self-care is business infrastructure.**

I'm now protecting it like I protect revenue.

## INTEGRATION v06
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: CONTAINER-BUILDING

I gave my compassion boundaries. Not because compassion is bad.

Because compassion without limits becomes contempt for myself.

Now I have time for helping and time for myself.

The time I hold for myself isn't negotiable.

It's not nice to have. It's necessary.

**Carry line: Compassion for others starts with myself.**

I'm finally starting with myself.

## INTEGRATION v07
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: THRESHOLD-AWARENESS

I know when I'm at my limit. It's a feeling in my chest. A heaviness.

When I notice it, I stop taking on new things.

I tell people: I'm at capacity.

They're usually understanding. And if they're not, that's information about them, not me.

By honoring my capacity, I'm honoring my commitment to actually being useful.

**Carry line: Capacity is not a weakness.**

It's the only honest place to work from.
