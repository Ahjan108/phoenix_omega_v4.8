## EXERCISE v01
Open your laptop. Don't open email. Don't open Slack.
Open a blank document.
Write three things that are true right now.
"My business exists. I exist. That matters."
"I've built something real. That's evidence."
"I'm tired but I'm still here. That counts."
Write three true things. Not optimistic things. Just true.
Read them back to yourself once.
Close the document. Don't save it.
You needed to hear the truth said out loud.
Depression tells you lies. This is you fighting back.
Tomorrow, write three different truths.
By week end, you'll have challenged the lie.
The lie is that nothing matters. It does.
You matter. Your existence is the evidence.

## EXERCISE v02
Sit somewhere. Anywhere. Close your eyes for five minutes.
Don't try to think positively. Don't try to change your mood.
Just sit. Let the darkness be there.
Depression tells you to hide the darkness.
Resisting the darkness makes it worse.
You're going to sit with it instead.
After five minutes, open your eyes.
You survived those five minutes.
You're still here. The darkness didn't consume you.
By allowing the darkness, you've taken away its power.
Darkness only has power when you fight it.
Do this once a day. Five minutes of surrender.
By week's end, five minutes will feel manageable.
By month's end, you'll realize the darkness has less grip.
You can't think your way out of depression.
But you can sit with it until it passes.

This system works because it removes the constant decision.

The system lets you rest.
