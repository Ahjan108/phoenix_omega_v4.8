## EXERCISE v01
Step 1: When you notice the 3 AM math spiral starting, pause. Name it. Say out loud: That's the business brain. Not the present brain.

Step 2: Move to a different room if you can. Not to work on it. To separate your nervous system from the bed equation.

Step 3: Feet on the floor. Cool surface. Name five things the floor is doing that the bed isn't. Stable. Supportive. Separate. Let your body register the difference before you go back.

## EXERCISE v02
Step 1: The pitch deck is playing on loop. Stop trying to ignore it. Write the three fears underneath it. Not the business fears. Your fears. The ones generating the loop.

Step 2: Next to each fear, write one true thing. Not reassurance. Truth. Something that was true three months ago and is still true now.

Step 3: Read the true things when the loop starts again. Your nervous system doesn't need the lies to work. It needs the pattern interrupted.

## EXERCISE v03
Step 1: Lie still for sixty seconds. Don't try to sleep. Just inventory your body. Where is the tension. Name each location like you're describing it to someone else.

Step 2: For each tension point, ask: Is this fear. Is this fatigue. Is this preparation for something. Let your body answer.

Step 3: Pick the largest tension. Tighten it harder on purpose for five seconds. Release. Repeat three times. Your nervous system needs to know you see it, not that you're fighting it.

## EXERCISE v04
Step 1: The what-if about the runway started during the day. Trace it back. When did you first feel the numbers were wrong. Was it data or was it a tone in someone's voice.

Step 2: Write down what actually happened in that moment versus what your nervous system decided it meant.

Step 3: Every time the what-if returns tonight, read the what-actually-happened first. Your brain needs the sequence corrected, not denied.

## EXERCISE v05
Step 1: You're not sleeping because your nervous system thinks work isn't done. Your nervous system is right. Work isn't done. Work is never done.

Step 2: Tell your nervous system explicitly: The business will survive if I sleep. Nothing gets worse if I rest. This is the only true statement that matters.

Step 3: Repeat this like a frequency, not like reassurance. Like you're retuning a radio. Not to convince yourself. To give your body a different instruction set.

## EXERCISE v06
Step 1: When the 2 AM fear arrives, don't fight the wakefulness. Set a timer for fifteen minutes. Stay awake on purpose.

Step 2: In those fifteen minutes, feel the fear without running from it. Let it exist. Your nervous system is not broken because it feels this.

Step 3: When the timer stops, permission to sleep or rest. Your body resets when it knows it's not being forced. Paradox but it works.
