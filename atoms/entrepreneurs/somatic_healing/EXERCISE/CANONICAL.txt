# entrepreneurs × somatic_healing × what the body held when the mind moved on
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Count: 30
# Notes: step-based (Step 1/2/3)
---
## EXERCISE v01
Step 1: Close your eyes. Scan from head to toes. Notice tension, tightness, numbness. Don't try to change it.

Step 2: Pick one place where tension lives. Put your hand there. Feel it. Ask: How long have you been holding this?

Step 3: Breathe into that space for three minutes. Not trying to release. Just acknowledging it's there.
---
## EXERCISE v02
Step 1: Lie down. One hand on chest, one on belly. Notice your breath without changing it.

Step 2: How much of your body is it reaching? Is it shallow? Full?

Step 3: For five minutes, exhale longer than you inhale. This signals safety to your nervous system.
---
## EXERCISE v03
Step 1: Stand in front of a mirror. Look at your shoulders. Are they raised? Are you braced?

Step 2: Consciously lower them. Notice the resistance. How much effort does it take to relax something that's braced?

Step 3: Every time you notice them raising again, just lower them again. Not fixing. Noticing.
---
## EXERCISE v04
Step 1: Identify one physical sensation you've been ignoring. Tight chest. Stiff neck. Shallow breathing.

Step 2: Don't fix it. Describe it. What does it feel like? Where does it start and end?

Step 3: Imagine the thing that created this tension. Your body carried this as protection. Thank it. You can rest now.
---
## EXERCISE v05
Step 1: Do something your body hasn't done in a while. Walk slowly. Stretch. Breathe deeply. Feel what comes up.

Step 2: Notice: What does this feel like? Does it feel good? Does it feel wrong or scary?

Step 3: If it feels wrong, that's information. Move slowly anyway. You're teaching your nervous system ease is possible.
---
## EXERCISE v06
Step 1: Find a position where your body feels mostly comfortable.

Step 2: Notice the places where it's still braced: jaw, shoulders, hands. Can you release any in this position?

Step 3: Spend ten minutes just noticing. Not changing. Your body will relax when it believes it's safe.
