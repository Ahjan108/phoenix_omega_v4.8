# first_responders × anxiety × the nervous system that forgot how to stand down
# Slot type: REFLECTION
# Count: 30

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Your body doesn't know how to reset. That's the mechanism. It learned escalation so well that the off switch disappeared. I watched mine go—took about six years before I realized the nervous system had become a one-way valve. Activation always possible. Deactivation never arrived.

On shift, that's adaptation. Off shift, it's a problem. You're at a dinner table and your hands shake. You're watching a movie and your heart races. The system learned to stay ready, and it never learned to stop. This isn't weakness. It's what happens when you teach the nervous system that danger arrives without warning.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I watched a medic's system lock itself into permanent vigilance. She could feel the shift happening—couldn't sit still, couldn't sleep past four a.m., couldn't be in a room without mapping exits. The protective mechanism was working exactly as designed. Keep you alive. Stay alert. Never fully relax.

What nobody tells you is that this protection becomes the prison. Your nervous system is trying to save you from something that isn't there anymore. The call ended. The shift's over. But the mechanism doesn't believe in clock time.

The invisibility is that it feels normal to you. Everyone at the station is the same way. It's just how responders are built. Until you step outside and realize most people don't live at a 7 or 8 on the activation scale as their baseline.
