# first_responders × boundaries × the always-available cost
# Slot type: REFLECTION
# Count: 30
# Voice: first-person author, teaching, no resolution
# Spec: 120–180 words, names mechanism, no fix delivered, open ending

---
## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The job doesn't teach you how to have boundaries. It teaches you to ignore them. Someone needs something? You respond. Even off shift. Especially off shift. Because the mechanism learned that your availability is the measure of your worth.

I didn't realize I'd become completely permeable until I looked at a calendar and couldn't find a single day that was actually mine. Every moment was allocated to someone else's need. The automatic mechanism had learned to sacrifice anything for responsiveness.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The protective mechanism is keeping other people safe by making yourself completely unavailable to yourself. This sounds noble until you realize that your nervous system has learned to treat other people's emergencies as more important than your own survival.

I watched someone I care about disappear into this. By the time they noticed, they'd said yes to everything. They couldn't say no if they wanted to.

---

You've become completely accessible. Completely obligated.

## REFLECTION v04
---

voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic

The automatic boundary-erasing mechanism starts with the job. You have to be available during shift. You have to respond to every call. Then it expands. You're on call off shift. You're covering for others. You're available to everyone.

Off the job, the mechanism keeps running. You can't distinguish between things you should do and things you want to do.
