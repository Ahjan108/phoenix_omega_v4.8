# first_responders × compassion_fatigue × the numbness that wasn't there at the start
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Count: 30

---
## EXERCISE v01
The feeling inventory. What have you stopped feeling.

Step 1: List five calls that used to matter. That used to cost you something emotionally. Write them down.

Step 2: For each one, write what you used to feel. Anger. Sadness. Urgency. Purpose. What was the feeling.

Step 3: Now check in. Do you still feel those things about similar calls. Or is there flatness now. This is your map of the numbness. You know what's missing because you remember what was there.

---
## EXERCISE v02
The one-minute care practice. Intentional compassion instead of autopilot.

Step 1: On your next call, at some point during the response, pause for one minute. Just one.

Step 2: During that minute, notice something human about the person you're helping. Not clinical. Human. What's one thing you can see about them that has nothing to do with why they called.

Step 3: That one minute is you accessing compassion. It's not going to save them. It's saving you. Repeated practice rewires the numbness.

---
## EXERCISE v03
The story-keeping practice. Don't let the calls disappear.

Step 1: Pick one call from this week. The one that would have mattered once. Write down two sentences about what happened.

Step 2: Keep these in a folder. Once a week, spend five minutes reading what you've written.

Step 3: You're training your memory to hold the calls. You're preventing them from sliding through. You're insisting they matter.

---
## EXERCISE v04
The debrief practice. Actually talk about the hard stuff.

Step 1: After a call that's heavy, don't joke. Find one person. One. Not the whole crew.

Step 2: Say one true thing about how the call affected you. One sentence. That person might do the same.

Step 3: You're breaking the pattern of numbing through humor. You're allowing feeling to exist even briefly.

---
## EXERCISE v05
The empathy reset. Deliberately access care for someone specific.

Step 1: Think of someone you love. Someone you still feel things about. Hold them in your mind.

Step 2: Now think of the person from the last call. Imagine they're also someone you love. What would you wish for them.

Step 3: You're resetting your brain's empathy circuit. You're using one connection to jumpstart another.

---
## EXERCISE v06
The gratitude toward people you help. Shift from numbness to acknowledgment.

Step 1: Who did you help this week that got better. Who had a different outcome because you were there.

Step 2: Take one minute. Acknowledge it. Not for them. For you. Acknowledge that what you do matters.

Step 3: Gratitude is the opposite of numbness. Repeated practice strengthens the capacity.

---
## EXERCISE v07
The name practice. Call people by their names instead of their conditions.

Step 1: On your next call, learn their name if you can. Use it. Say it out loud.

Step 2: At the end, remember the name. Not the call. The person.

Step 3: Names restore humanity. They're an antidote to the flatness that comes from treating people as problems to solve.

---
## EXERCISE v08
The boundary between self-protection and self-abandonment. Know the difference.

Step 1: Healthy self-protection means caring and still staying functional. Identify one call where you were both.

Step 2: Identify one call where you shut down completely. That's not protection. That's abandonment.

Step 3: You're learning the difference. Protection lets you feel and still act. Abandonment is when the feeling doesn't exist at all.

---
## EXERCISE v09
The energy audit for emotional energy. Where is your compassion going.

Step 1: Where do you have energy to care. Work. Family. Friends. Self. Write it down.

Step 2: Look at the list. Are the people you help on this list. If not, that's where the numbness is coming from.

Step 3: You need to get yourself back on the list. That's not selfish. That's how you rebuild capacity to care about anyone else.

---
## EXERCISE v10
The mirror conversation. Talk to yourself about what you've lost.

Step 1: Stand in the mirror. Look at yourself. Say one true thing about how the job has changed you.

Step 2: Then say one true thing about what you still want to feel. What's worth fighting to keep.

Step 3: You're not trying to fix the numbness in the mirror. You're acknowledging it. Acknowledgment is the first step to recovery.

---
## EXERCISE v11
The call that mattered conversation. Find one from years ago and remember why it mattered.

Step 1: Think back. Identify a call that broke you emotionally. One that cost you sleep. One that changed you.

Step 2: What made it matter. What was it about that call that touched something real in you.

Step 3: You're accessing the part of yourself that still knows how to care deeply. It's still there. It's just buried.

---
## EXERCISE v12
The small kindness practice. Intentional care in tiny doses.

Step 1: Tomorrow, do one small thing that requires care. Actually care. Could be helping someone. Could be noticing something.

Step 2: Notice what it feels like to be intentional about it instead of automatic.

Step 3: Repeated small acts of intentional care rebuild the capacity for larger caring.

---
## EXERCISE v13
The comparison journal. Don't compare your numbness to the past, compare it to where you used to be worst.

Step 1: Write down a time when the numbness was deepest. When you didn't feel anything at all.

Step 2: Compare it to now. Is it slightly better. Slightly worse. The same.

Step 3: You're measuring change. Small improvements matter. They're evidence that numbness isn't permanent.

---
## EXERCISE v14
The crew conversation about feeling. Normalize talking about the numbness.

Step 1: Bring it up. Not complaint. Just conversation. I'm noticing I'm numb to some of this. Has anyone felt that.

Step 2: Most people have. Normalizing the experience makes it less isolating.

Step 3: The isolation of the numbness is part of what deepens it. Sharing it breaks that isolation.

---
## EXERCISE v15
The purpose reconnection. Remember why you took the job.

Step 1: Write down why you started. The reason. Not the job description. The reason.

Step 2: Read it. Sit with it. Is that reason still valid. Is there a version of that reason that still works.

Step 3: You're not going back to who you were. You're finding a version of purpose that works with who you've become.

---
## EXERCISE v16
The feeling practice. Deliberately feel something about something small.

Step 1: Put on music that moves you. A song. Anything. Just five minutes.

Step 2: Let yourself feel whatever comes. Don't judge it. Don't suppress it.

Step 3: You're rewiring your capacity for feeling. You're proving to yourself that you can still access emotion.

---
## EXERCISE v17
The person follow-up practice. Know what happened to someone you helped.

Step 1: Identify one person you helped. Try to find out if they got better.

Step 2: If you can know, know. If you can't, imagine a good outcome. Completion matters.

Step 3: Your brain needs to know that helping leads somewhere. Incomplete calls lead to numbness.

---
## EXERCISE v18
The compassion limit reality check. What's actually sustainable.

Step 1: If you had unlimited compassion, how would you show up differently at work and home.

Step 2: What small version of that is actually sustainable. Not all of it. A version.

Step 3: You're not trying to go back to unlimited caring. You're finding what you can genuinely sustain.

---
## EXERCISE v19
The hardness assessment. Is the hardening protective or destructive.

Step 1: Where has hardening helped you. Where has it hurt.

Step 2: Is there any softness you want to keep. Any softness worth protecting.

Step 3: You're not trying to go soft everywhere. You're choosing where hardness makes sense and where it's cost you too much.

---
## EXERCISE v20
The call that worked out. Celebrate the wins even if you don't feel them.

Step 1: Identify a call with a good outcome. Someone you helped who got better.

Step 2: Tell the story to someone. Even if you don't feel the joy, tell it. Let the teller experience it.

Step 3: The feeling might return through storytelling. Narratives can resurrect emotion.

---
## EXERCISE v21
The connection practice. Intentional presence with one person this week.

Step 1: Pick someone you care about. Not the job. Someone at home or a friend.

Step 2: Spend 20 minutes with them where you're actually present. Not thinking about work. Actually here.

Step 3: You're teaching your nervous system that presence and feeling are possible. You're rebuilding the capacity.

---
## EXERCISE v22
The long view. Where has the numbness protected you.

Step 1: Write down the hard calls that you survived because you could numb out. Because you didn't feel the full weight.

Step 2: That numbness saved you then. Ask yourself if it's still serving you now.

Step 3: It might still be serving part of the time. You're getting accurate about when and where.

---
## EXERCISE v23
The witness practice. Watch yourself experiencing numbness and observe it without judgment.

Step 1: During a call that would have affected you once, notice the flatness. Don't fight it.

Step 2: Just observe. Oh, there's the numbness. That's what that feels like right now.

Step 3: You're creating space between you and the numbness. That space is where change happens.

---
## EXERCISE v24
The loss acknowledgment. Name what the job has taken.

Step 1: Write down three things the job has cost you. Softness. Hope. Connection. Whatever's real.

Step 2: These are losses. They're real. Acknowledge them.

Step 3: You can't grieve what you won't acknowledge. Acknowledgment is necessary.

## EXERCISE v25
The station memorial or moment. Be present for something meaningful.

Step 1: The next time your station marks something important. A loss. An anniversary. Show up intentionally.

Step 2: Try to feel something. If you can't, just notice. Just be there.

Step 3: Being present for meaningful things is training ground for accessing deeper feeling.

## EXERCISE v26
The mentor conversation. Ask someone more experienced how they stayed connected.

Step 1: Find someone who's been on the job longer and hasn't gone fully numb. Ask them how.

Step 2: Listen for what they did. What they protect. What boundaries they set.

Step 3: You're getting evidence that connection is still possible. You're getting a map from someone who found it.

## EXERCISE v27
The small win journal. Record one moment per week when you felt something.

Step 1: Each week, write down one moment when emotion showed up. Could be brief. Could be small.

Step 2: It could be sadness. Anger. Joy. Anything. The capacity is there.

Step 3: You're gathering evidence that feeling isn't completely gone. It's just dormant. Evidence builds hope.

---
## EXERCISE v28
The realistic hope practice. Hope without naivety.

Step 1: I don't think everything will get better. That's not where you are. But what's one small thing that could improve.

Step 2: Focus there. Not on total recovery. On one small improvement.

Step 3: Small hope is more sustainable than big hope. Small improvements build.

---
## EXERCISE v29
The separation practice. Work feelings don't have to follow you everywhere.

Step 1: Create a physical transition between work and home. Could be a shower. Could be a drive ritual.

Step 2: During that transition, consciously leave the numbness at work. You don't have to bring it home.

Step 3: You're teaching your system that feeling-state doesn't have to be constant. You can be numb at work and present at home.

---
## EXERCISE v30
The one true feeling practice. Find one thing you still feel about the job.

Step 1: Beneath the numbness, is there one emotion that's still alive about this work. Anger at injustice. Pride in something you did. Anything.

Step 2: Sit with that one feeling. It's the ember. Don't try to build it into a fire yet.

Step 3: You're not trying to restore full compassion. You're tending the one thing that's still alive. That's enough.
