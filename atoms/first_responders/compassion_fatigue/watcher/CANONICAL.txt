path: Present in body, gone in mind
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
You're doing the job. Your hands know where everything is. Your body runs the scene. But the part of you that's supposed to care about the outcome is somewhere else. Like you're watching yourself from the bay ceiling. The victim is in front of you. Your crew is responding. The protocols are executing. But the emotional connection is severed. You're numb. Not in the way that feels like protection. In the way that feels like absence. You notice it most on the calls that should break you open. The ones where your crew is clearly affected. And you're just there. Doing the work.

path: The professional mask that stuck
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
Professional distance. That's what they teach you. Don't let the calls get personal. Keep your edge. Stay detached. It's good advice for the first few years. Keeps you from making decisions based on emotion. But somewhere along the way, the distance became permanent. You can put on the professional mask and you're not sure you can take it off anymore. Even at home. Even with people you love. The watcher state is where you live now. Observing from behind glass. Still functional. Still performing. But emotionally absent.

Here's the thing about being a watcher. It doesn't feel bad most of the time. It feels safe. You're not vulnerable. You're not emotionally invested.
