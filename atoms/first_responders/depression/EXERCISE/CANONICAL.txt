## EXERCISE v01
Step 1: Name one specific task you do on shift automatically. Something you could do in your sleep. Notice it for one full day without trying to feel anything about it.

Step 2: After the shift, sit for five minutes and list what you physically did. Not the story. The actions. Grip. Turn. Lift. Drive. Write it like an equipment checklist.

Step 3: Read it back. You didn't do nothing. Your body worked. That's data. That's proof the engine's still running even when the gauge is broken.

Step 3: Your nervous system remembers. It locked this into your body for a reason. You're not broken. You're in storage. That's different.

## EXERCISE v04

Step 3: Log it. You're teaching your nervous system that variation exists. That change is possible. That you can still make choices even when the world feels set to one speed.
