SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
You're grieving someone you used to be. Not because they got old or moved away. Because the job changed them. The person who took this position seven years ago had a different internal landscape. SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
Joy used to be available to you. Easily. You remember it. The job gave you joy. The brotherhood. The purpose. The feeling of being needed. The feeling of making a difference. SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
You could sleep twelve hours and still wake up tired. That's the grief of depression. It's not about the hours. It's about the weight of living with a darkened world. Everything takes more effort when the light is dim. Lifting your body is harder. Making decisions is harder. Even small conversations are harder because there's less energy available. The grief is in recognizing that something essential in your operating system has changed. The engine is idling at a lower temperature.
