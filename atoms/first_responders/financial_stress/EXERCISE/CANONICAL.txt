## EXERCISE v01
Step 1: Write down the exact salary you're making right now. Not what you thought you'd be making. The actual number.

Step 2: Write down the cost of your essential living expenses for one month. Rent, utilities, food, insurance. Not luxuries. Essentials.

Step 3: Subtract. Look at what's left. That's the actual problem you're solving for. You're not failing. The math is failing. That's different. Name it correctly.

## EXERCISE v02
Step 1: List every second job, overtime shift, and side gig you're working or considering working.

Step 2: Calculate what percentage of your weekly time that represents. What percentage of your energy. What percentage of your body.

Step 3: That's the cost that doesn't show up in the budget. You're paying in pieces of yourself. Name it. Own it. That's real data.

## EXERCISE v03
Step 1: Identify one medical or dental thing you're not addressing because you can't afford it. Write it down.

Step 2: Estimate the cost of addressing it now versus the cost of letting it get worse. What's the math.

Step 3: You're not being irresponsible. You're being strategic with limited resources. That's survival, not failure. Know the difference.

## EXERCISE v04

## EXERCISE v05
Step 1: Calculate your actual hourly wage. Divide your salary by the hours you actually work. Include commute if you're not paid for it.

Step 2: Look at that number. That's what your time is worth to the system.

Step 3: That number is information. It's not your value. Your value isn't determined by the system. But this number is the system's offer. Know what you're being paid. Know if it's fair. Most likely it's not.

Step 3: You're being asked to subsidize your own employment. That's information. That's not normal. Name it. Decide if you're okay with it.
