## STORY v01
---
path: The numbness after the loss
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
Right after it happened, people expected you to grieve visibly. To be devastated. Instead you felt nothing. Shock, maybe. But not grief. The loss was too big. Your nervous system shutdown the feeling capacity so the pain wouldn't take you offline. You became a watcher. Present at your own loss. Observing the people around you react. But separate from the reaction. The emotional processing was happening somewhere you couldn't access. You were outside looking in at your own devastation.

SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
People tell you that the grief will come. That the numbness is temporary. That the loss will hit you eventually. You believe them. You're waiting for it. But it doesn't come. You're in the watcher state and the watcher state is protective. The grief is there but it's behind glass. You can see its outline. You can understand conceptually that you should be devastated. ## STORY v04

path: The moment when it finally hit
BAND: 2
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
It wasn't at the funeral. It wasn't the first week. It was three months later when you were in the grocery store and you saw something that reminded you of them. Just a small thing. Something they would have said something about. And suddenly the watcher state cracked. The glass broke. And the grief came through so fast it took your breath. You had to leave your shopping cart. You had to sit in the truck for twenty minutes. That's when you realized the numbness wasn't protecting you. It was just delaying.
