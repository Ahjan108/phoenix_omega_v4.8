## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
A boundary is not a wall. It's a line that says: I care about you and I also need things from me. It says: what I'm willing to give and what I'm not. When you don't have boundaries, you give until you're empty. Then you resent the people you were trying to help. Boundaries are how you stay kind without disappearing.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
You say yes when you want to say no. You stay late when you want to leave. You listen to someone complain for an hour when you're exhausted. Then you feel angry and trapped. That anger is information: you've crossed your own boundary. The boundary exists; you just haven't said it out loud yet.

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
Someone asks for something. Your first instinct is to say yes immediately. But what if you pause and ask yourself: do I actually want to do this? Can I do this without resentment? What would I need for this to feel okay? That pause is where boundaries live. It's not about being difficult. It's about being honest.
