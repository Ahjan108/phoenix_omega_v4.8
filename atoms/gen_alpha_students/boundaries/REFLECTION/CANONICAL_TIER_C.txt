# gen_alpha_students × boundaries × REFLECTION
# Slot type: REFLECTION
# Count: 30

---

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

You cannot choose boundaries you have never experienced. The capacity develops through modeling, reinforcement, and respect. If no one ever showed you what a boundary looks like, the mechanism learned a different architecture instead. Everything is available. Everything is negotiable. Everything can be demanded. Now when someone asks for something, your first response is yes. You do not even realize refusal was an option.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: C

Boundaries are not chosen. They are learned through experience. When someone violates your space and faces no consequence, you learn: space is negotiable. When refusal is punished, you learn: no is dangerous. The mechanism absorbs these lessons without your consent. Many students grew up with adults who could not maintain their own boundaries. So the students learned boundarylessness instead. Now you do not know which requests are reasonable to deny. You do not know you are allowed to have preferences that differ from what others want. The mechanism never learned: this is mine, this is not.
