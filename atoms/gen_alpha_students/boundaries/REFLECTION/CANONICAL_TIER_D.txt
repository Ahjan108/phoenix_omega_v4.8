## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: D

I have noticed that boundaries are often presented as something you should have, as if they are a choice. In reality, the capacity to set a boundary is something that develops through having had boundaries modeled, reinforced, and respected. You cannot choose to have boundaries if you have never experienced them. There is a mechanism that learns what is negotiable and what is not negotiable through experience. And learning it is slow and often painful.
---
