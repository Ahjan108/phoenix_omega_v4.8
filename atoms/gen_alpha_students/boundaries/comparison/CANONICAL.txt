And Jaden is learning something about himself. Something about whether his needs matter or whether other people's visible behavior is the real measure of worth. Maya's phone never stops. Her friends share their accomplishments every hour. Every hour. A perfect score. A role in the play. A new follower count. A college acceptance. It's showing her all the ways she's falling behind. All the ways her 96 means nothing. All the ways her small accomplishments are dust in comparison to their glitter. She can't breathe anymore. The comparison has taken all the air. She's convinced that she's falling further every single day. That the distance between her and her friends is becoming impossible. That she'll never catch up. The measurement has become her entire sense of self. She's defined by what she hasn't achieved. She's defined by the gap. She's become the gap. Her worth has disappeared into it completely. She's lying awake at night doing the math. Trying to calculate the exact distance. Trying to figure out if it's possible to close it. Knowing it's not.
