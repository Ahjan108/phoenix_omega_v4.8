## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya maps the shame's vocabulary. The shame has specific words for boundary-setting: selfish, mean, bad friend, not nice. The words are all character-level accusations. The shame doesn't say: "Saying no to this specific request is problematic." The shame says: "You are the kind of person who says no." The escalation from action to identity is instant. Setting one boundary becomes being a selfish person. The identity-level accusation is harder to resist than the action-level one because the action-level one can be evaluated. The identity-level one bypasses evaluation and goes straight to shame. She's not evaluating a decision. She's defending her entire character.

## STORY v21
---
path: small social awkwardness
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
Zoe said something weird in class. Not offensive. Not wrong. Just slightly off. Her timing was wrong. Everyone looked at her for a second. It was a tiny moment. A half-second. Maybe they didn't even notice. Zoe noticed. Her face warmed. The heat is just a slight flush. Not a full blush. Just warmth spreading up her neck. Something is shifting in her body. The shame is arriving quietly. It doesn't roar. It whispers. Did she say that weird? She's replaying the moment in her mind. She's watching the other kids' faces in slow motion. The half-second of their attention feels longer now. It feels like judgment. Probably she imagined the judgment. Probably they're already thinking about something else. But Zoe is still thinking about it. The shame is so quiet she almost doesn't feel it. It's just a small weight in her chest. A tiny stone placed on her ribs. She'll probably forget this happened by tomorrow. Probably. But right now the shame is settling in. It's quiet. It's small. It's barely there. Almost. Zoe tries to refocus on the lesson but her mind keeps replaying. What if everyone thinks she's weird. What if someone remembers. What if that moment defines how people see her. The shame doesn't have evidence. It just has fear. It just has the echo of that half-second look. And that's enough to install itself. The mechanism is so subtle she doesn't realize it's happening. She won't speak as much in class anymore.

Failure. His body is now a problem. His voice is now a betrayal. He walks through the hallway and he's hyperaware of his voice. He's monitoring it. Controlling it. Trying to make sure it doesn't crack again. The shame has made him conscious of something that was automatic. Now it's impossible. He speaks less in classes now. He avoids presentations. He tries to become smaller. Quieter. Less visible. He's learned that his body can betray him at any moment. That his voice can expose him. That his physical form is evidence of his inadequacy. The shame is installing the watcher mechanism. Now he's watching himself constantly. Monitoring for the next betrayal. The next sign that he's fundamentally flawed. He's reviewing every conversation thinking about whether his voice cracked. He's becoming hypervigilant about his own body.
