# gen_alpha_students × compassion_fatigue × compression_distillation
# Slot type: COMPRESSION
# Count: 30
# Voice: present tense, one insight, no lecturing
# Spec: 40–120 words, 2–6 sentences, one insight, one reframe, TTS-safe

---
## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
You care deeply about people. You listen when they're struggling. You show up. Then you find yourself exhausted—not physically tired, but emotionally empty. You've absorbed so much of their pain that there's nothing left for you. That emptiness is compassion fatigue. It's what happens when you give without refilling yourself. You can care and also need to take care of yourself.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
Everyone comes to you with their problems. You're the fixer, the listener, the one who cares. And gradually you realize: you have problems too. But there's no space for them because you're always focused on someone else. Compassion fatigue shows up as resentment, numbness, or the feeling that you don't even have a self anymore. That's the signal: you need to tend to your own garden.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
You hear sad news and you can't feel sad anymore. You hear about someone's crisis and you feel numb instead of caring. That numbness isn't cruelty; it's exhaustion. Your emotional system has turned off protection mode. The reframe: you're not broken or bad. You're flooded. You need to empty the bucket before you can care again.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
compression_family: C4
---
You take on others' emotions like they're your own. Your friend is anxious and suddenly you're anxious. Your parent is disappointed and you feel the disappointment in your chest. That's empathy, which is beautiful. But it's also unsustainable if you're not distinguishing between their feelings and yours. Their sadness is not your sadness. You can witness it without carrying it.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---
compression_family: C5
---
Compassion fatigue is partly about responsibility. You feel like you need to fix people, to make them feel better, to solve their problems. But you can't. And that inability creates this pressure that wears you down. The liberation: you can care about someone and not be responsible for their wellbeing. Those are two separate things.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---
compression_family: C1
---
You stop reading the news because there's too much suffering. You avoid certain people because their problems are too heavy. You notice yourself being cynical when you used to be hopeful. That's compassion fatigue. It's your system saying: I can't absorb any more pain. Listen to that. It's not weakness. It's honesty about your limits.

---

The fatigue comes partly from taking on responsibility that isn't yours. What if you could be present without being the fixer?

Because eventually you'll be empty, and then you won't be helpful to anyone, including yourself. The reframe: your worth is not about your usefulness. It's just there.
