## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: B

Compassion fatigue arrives quietly while you are busy being loyal. You are the one who holds space. The one who stays present. The one who listens when others need listening to. The mechanism was designed to be affected by what you witness. But the witness has no outlet. You keep accumulating pain. The suffering you absorb from your friends does not drain through you. It stays. The emotional energy flows in one direction: toward everyone else. There is no refill. The mechanism celebrates your caring while it depletes you. Eventually you are drowning in the accumulated care. You become exhausted from the weight of holding space for a generation carrying unprecedented stress.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: B

You became the friend people text when things fall apart. You have been listening, present, steady. The mechanism celebrates your capacity to care. And then one day you realize there is no one listening to you. You are running on empty. There is a mechanism called the caring collapse, and what it does is deplete the very capacity it is trying to sustain. You have spent your emotional energy on other people. You have given your attention to other people's problems. You have made room in your mind for other people's struggles. The room you made has taken all the available space. What you come to understand is that this mechanism is particularly active in environments where there is ambient suffering. Your friends are struggling. There is a pandemic. There is political instability. There is uncertainty about the future. The mechanism that makes you a good friend is the same mechanism that can run you into the ground. Because the suffering does not run out. There is always more.
