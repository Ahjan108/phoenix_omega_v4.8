## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

Compassion fatigue arrives quietly while you are busy being a good friend. You are the steady one, the listener, the person who holds space. And then one day you realize there is no one listening to you. You are running on empty. The mechanism that makes you caring is the same mechanism that can run you into the ground. Because the suffering does not end. There is always someone who needs listening to. There is always more pain to hold.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: C

You became the friend people text at 2am. You listen. You are present. You make room for their suffering. The mechanism celebrates your loyalty and capacity to care. But the room you made has taken all the available space. There is nothing left for you. The suffering does not drain through you. It accumulates. Your friends are struggling. There is a pandemic. There is political instability. There is uncertainty about the future. The mechanism was designed to care about one person's pain. It was not designed for the constant ambient suffering of a generation.
