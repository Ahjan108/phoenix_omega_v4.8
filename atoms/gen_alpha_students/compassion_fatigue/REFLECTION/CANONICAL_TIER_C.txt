## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

I have noticed that compassion fatigue often arrives quietly. You have been the person your friends text when things fall apart. You have been listening. You have been present. You have been the steady one. And then one day you realize that there is no one listening to you. That you are not present to yourself. That you are not steady anymore. You are running on empty. There is a mechanism that is called the caring collapse, and what it does is deplete the very capacity it is trying to sustain. You have spent your emotional energy on other people. You have given your attention to other people's problems. You have made room in your mind for other people's struggles.

---

voice_mode: companion

I have noticed that compassion fatigue often arrives alongside the realization that you cannot fix anything. Your friend has an anxiety disorder, and listening to you will not cure it. Your parent is unhappy, and understanding them will not make them happy. Your classmate is lonely, and your friendship will not solve the loneliness. The suffering continues regardless of how much you care. There is a mechanism that believes that caring enough, listening hard enough, understanding deeply enough, will somehow create an outcome. That if you just hold it all with enough attention, something will shift. That your presence will heal. But the presence does not heal. The people still hurt. The problems still exist. The suffering continues.
