## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
Courage isn't the absence of fear. It's doing the thing while you're afraid. You give the presentation while your hands shake. You join the group while your stomach is tight. You speak up while your voice wavers. The fear doesn't go away. You act anyway. That's what courage actually is: action in the face of genuine fear. You don't have to feel brave; you have to act brave.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
You're waiting to feel ready before you do something hard. You're waiting for confidence to arrive. But confidence comes after you do the thing, not before. The sequence is backwards from what you think. You act, you survive, you feel stronger, then confidence arrives. Courage is the bridge between fear and confidence.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
You're afraid of failing. So you don't try. You stay safe. But staying safe is also a kind of failure—it's failing to become who you could be. The reframe: failure is information, not verdict. If you fail, you learn. If you don't try, you learn nothing. Courage is choosing the learning over the safety.
---

It's letting that voice get louder than the protection voice. It's worth it.
