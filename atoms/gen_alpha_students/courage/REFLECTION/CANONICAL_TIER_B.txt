## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: B

I have noticed that courage is often presented as the absence of fear. But the people who are actually courageous are often quite frightened. They are frightened and they do it anyway. The fear does not disappear. The fear just becomes something that is present alongside the action. There is a mechanism that has been taught to interpret fear as a stop sign. If you are afraid, you should not do the thing. Fear means the thing is dangerous. Fear means you should protect yourself by not trying. But the mechanism is operating under a false assumption. Fear can coexist with possibility. Fear can exist alongside action. What I have come to understand is that the mechanism would be updated if it could understand that fear is information, not direction. Fear is telling you something about what matters to you, what you care about, what you have to lose. But fear is not telling you to stop. Fear is just telling you that something matters. The courage that is visible in the world is almost never the absence of fear.

I have noticed that the courage to speak in class is much harder when you are afraid of being wrong. The mechanism is not afraid of public speaking in general. The mechanism is afraid of saying something incorrect, of being corrected, of being shown to be mistaken in front of people. There is a mechanism that has learned to associate wrongness with exposure and shame. If you say something wrong, people will see that you do not know. People will remember. People will judge. The mechanism is protecting you from exposure by preventing you from speaking. What I have come to understand is that the mechanism is based on a kind of absolute belief in the permanence of mistakes. If you say something wrong once, you are forever the person who said that thing wrong. But the reality is much more forgiving. People forget. People understand that mistakes are part of learning. People are often much less focused on your mistake than you are. The paradox is what keeps the mechanism stuck.
