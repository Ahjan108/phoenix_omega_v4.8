## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: C

I have noticed that courage is often presented as the absence of fear. But the people who are actually courageous are often quite frightened. They are frightened and they do it anyway. The fear does not disappear. The fear just becomes something that is present alongside the action. There is a mechanism that has been taught to interpret fear as a stop sign. If you are afraid, you should not do the thing. Fear means the thing is dangerous. Fear means you should protect yourself by not trying. But the mechanism is operating under a false assumption. Fear can coexist with possibility. Fear can exist alongside action. They are companions.

I have noticed that the courage to speak in class is much harder when you are afraid of being wrong. The mechanism is not afraid of public speaking in general. The mechanism is afraid of saying something incorrect, of being corrected, of being shown to be mistaken in front of people. There is a mechanism that has learned to associate wrongness with exposure and shame. If you say something wrong, people will see that you do not know. People will remember. People will judge. The mechanism is protecting you from exposure by preventing you from speaking. What I have come to understand is that the mechanism is based on a kind of absolute belief in the permanence of mistakes.

## REFLECTION v30
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

I have noticed that courage is not about not being afraid. Courage is about moving toward something that matters to you while you are afraid. Courage is about recognizing that the fear is part of the process, not a stop sign. Courage is about doing the thing anyway. There is a mechanism that has been taught to interpret fear as a warning. But the mechanism could be updated to understand that some fear is protective and some fear is just the nervousness of trying something that matters. Some fear is a warning about genuine danger, and some fear is just the fear of being exposed. That is what courage actually is.
