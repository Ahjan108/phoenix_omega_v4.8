## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: D

I have noticed that courage is often presented as the absence of fear. But the people who are actually courageous are often quite frightened. They are frightened and they do it anyway. The fear does not disappear. The fear just becomes something that is present alongside the action. There is a mechanism that has been taught to interpret fear as a stop sign. If you are afraid, you should not do the thing. Fear means the thing is dangerous.
