## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
Depression is not sadness. Sadness is about something. Depression is about nothing. You wake up and the world is gray. Things you used to love feel empty. You feel numb instead of hurt. It's like someone turned the volume down on life. It's not weakness and it's not your fault. It's your brain chemistry, and it can change.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
You're supposed to be fine. Your life looks fine. But inside you feel heavy and slow and like there's no point. You feel guilty for feeling bad when you have nothing to feel bad about. But depression doesn't wait for a good reason. It just arrives. And when it does, you're not weak—you're sick. That's different.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
You're moving through the world like you're underwater. Everything takes effort. Getting up takes effort. Speaking takes effort. Caring takes effort. And you feel ashamed of how much effort it all takes. But depression slows everything down. That's not laziness. That's a symptom. When you treat it as a symptom instead of a character flaw, it becomes manageable.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
compression_family: C4
---
Everyone expects you to be happy. They want you to smile, to be positive, to cheer up. But you can't manufacture happiness when your brain is telling you there's nothing to be happy about. The shame of not feeling what you're supposed to feel can be bigger than the depression itself. But depression is real. It's medical. It needs treatment, not willpower.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---
compression_family: C5
---
You used to have interests. You used to enjoy things. Now you feel empty when you think about those things. You don't care about stuff that mattered. That emptiness is scary because it feels permanent. But it's not. Depression is a temporary hardening of the brain. With help, with time, the world can soften again.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---
compression_family: C1
---
You're isolating because being around people is exhausting. Being around yourself is exhausting. Everything feels like too much. So you withdraw. And the withdrawing makes it worse. The isolation deepens the depression. But knowing that—knowing the pattern—gives you one small tool: reach out to one person, even though every cell is saying hide.

## COMPRESSION v07
---
compression_family: C2
---
You're sleeping too much or not at all. You're eating too much or not at all. Your body is out of rhythm. That chaos is part of depression. It's not something you're doing wrong. Your nervous system is disregulated. The path back isn't willpower. It's help. It's treatment. It's time and support.

## COMPRESSION v08

compression_family: C3

People recover from depression. You can too. The moment you believe that—really believe it—something shifts. It's not instant. But it's the beginning.
