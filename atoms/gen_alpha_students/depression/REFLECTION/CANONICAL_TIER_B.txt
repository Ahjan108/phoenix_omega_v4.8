## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: B

The nothing does not announce itself. It arrives as background, as muted color, as distant sound. The mechanism has turned down the volume on your entire life. Everything that was supposed to matter feels like it does not. You are present and absent at the same time. The mechanism is protecting you by turning everything off. But the protection comes at a very high cost. The cost is that you are not really alive. You move through your day in a kind of silence. You do the things that are required. You show up. You function. But underneath the functioning, there is an absence. There is a kind of disconnection from everything that is supposed to matter. The mechanism has made survival possible. But the survival is incomplete.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: B

Depression in students often does not look like sadness. It looks like nothing. It looks like going through the day without feeling much of anything. It looks like getting good grades and being fine and not really being present in any of it. There is a mechanism called anhedonia, and what it does is flat. The mechanism turns off the sense of interest. The mechanism turns off the sense of pleasure. The mechanism turns off the capacity to feel moved by things. The result is a kind of walking through the world that looks like functioning but is actually a kind of absence. What you come to understand is that depression is often invisible because it looks like okay. You are doing the things. You are going to school. You are getting decent grades. You are showing up. No one is concerned because you are not falling apart. You are just not really there. The mechanism that is creating this flatness is not sadness. Sadness is a response to loss. The flatness is the absence of feeling.
