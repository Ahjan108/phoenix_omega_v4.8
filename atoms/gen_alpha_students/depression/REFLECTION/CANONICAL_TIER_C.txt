I have noticed that depression in students often does not look like sadness. It looks like nothing. It looks like going through the day without feeling much of anything. It looks like getting good grades and being fine and not really being present in any of it. There is a mechanism called anhedonia, and what it does is flat. The mechanism turns off the sense of interest. The mechanism turns off the sense of pleasure. The mechanism turns off the capacity to feel moved by things. And the result is a kind of walking through the world that looks like functioning but is actually a kind of absence. The cost is that you are not really alive.
