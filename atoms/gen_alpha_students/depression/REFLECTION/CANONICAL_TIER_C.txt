## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: C

The nothing does not announce itself. It is quiet. It looks like being fine. But underneath the fine, there is a kind of disconnection from everything that is supposed to matter. Colors are less bright. Sounds are distant. The mechanism has turned down the volume on your entire life. You are present and absent at the same time. The mechanism is protecting you by turning everything off. The cost is that you are not really alive.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: C

Depression in students often looks like nothing. It looks like okay. You are getting decent grades, showing up, doing what is required. But underneath, there is an absence. The mechanism has turned off the interest, the pleasure, the capacity to feel moved by things. You are walking through the world but not really there. The flatness is not sadness. Sadness is a response to loss. The flatness is the absence of feeling. The mechanism is not making you feel bad. It is making you feel nothing.
