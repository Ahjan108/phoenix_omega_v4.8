Zoe attends her Environmental Club meeting and raises her hand to suggest a campus cleanup initiative. Her voice sounds like her voice. Her hands gesture the way they normally gesture. She has thought about the cleanup and the suggestion is real. But she is watching herself suggest it. She sees the version of herself speaking and observing that version simultaneously. The club president nods and asks her to organize it. Zoe says yes. She hears herself say yes. She does not feel anything about it. The coffee she is holding is warm but she does not taste it. The room is fluorescent but she does not see it. She is behind something. She is observing her own life from a distance that she cannot name or close. This is not dissociation. It is not dramatic. It is just the feeling of being present and not present at the same time. She goes home and types the cleanup details into her phone. She is a person organizing a cleanup. She watches herself type.
