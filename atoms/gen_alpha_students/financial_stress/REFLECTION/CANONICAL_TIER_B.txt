## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: B

The awareness that the future has a price arrives when you are still young. You become aware that money defines possibility. You become aware that survival is not cheap. The mechanism calculates the cost of being alive before you have lived very much. You have not earned your first dollar and already you feel the weight of needing to be financially responsible. The inherited anxiety becomes your anxiety. The burden arrives when you are still supposed to have possibilities. You start to believe it is your job to manage the financial stress that was never your job to manage. The mechanism turns the future into a problem to solve. You should be having possibility. Instead you are carrying the awareness of scarcity.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: B

Financial anxiety arrives silently before you have any money of your own. You do not have bills you cannot pay. You just have an awareness that things cost. That your family is managing resources. That money is not unlimited. That the future has a price tag. There is a mechanism that is absorbing financial anxiety from the environment. You overhear a conversation about college costs. You notice your parents being stressed about bills. You become aware that money is a thing that runs out. The mechanism is internalizing the scarcity before you have even earned your first dollar. What you come to understand is that the financial anxiety is often not your own anxiety about your own money. The financial anxiety is inherited anxiety. The anxiety is coming from the adults around you. The anxiety is the message that money is tight, that resources are limited, that the future is expensive. You start to carry it. You start to feel responsible for it.
