## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: B

Grief becomes private because the world does not pause for it. You hold the loss in the spaces between classes, in quiet moments, in the night when you are alone. The mechanism is allowing you to survive. But the survival is incomplete. The mechanism is allowing you to function, but the functioning is hollow. You are here, but you are not fully here. You are present, but you are also absent. The mechanism is keeping you moving while your insides are still. What comes to matter is the performance of okayness. The grief becomes silent. No one knows about the breaking because you have become very good at keeping going. The cost is that no one sees. The cost is that you grieve alone.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: B

Student grief is often invisible because you are supposed to keep going. Your grandparent dies and you still have to go to school. Your friend moves away and you still have to show up for class. Your future changes and you still have to do your homework. The world does not pause while you grieve. There is a mechanism called stoic continuation, and what it does is allow you to function while you are broken. The mechanism knows that grief is happening. The mechanism knows that loss has occurred. But the mechanism keeps moving. The mechanism keeps doing. What you come to understand is that the stoic continuation is both a gift and a curse. The gift is that you are able to function. The curse is that functioning while grieving is profoundly lonely. No one sees that you are broken because you are still showing up. The visible performance of okayness prevents visible grief.
