I have noticed that student grief is often invisible because you are supposed to keep going. Your grandparent dies and you still have to go to school. Your friend moves away and you still have to show up for class. Your future changes and you still have to do your homework. The world does not pause while you grieve. There is a mechanism called stoic continuation, and what it does is allow you to function while you are broken. The mechanism knows that grief is happening. The mechanism knows that loss has occurred. But the mechanism keeps moving. The mechanism keeps doing. What I have come to understand is that the stoic continuation is both a gift and a curse. The gift is that you are able to function. The curse is that functioning while grieving is profoundly lonely. No one sees that you are broken because you are still showing up. What I have come to think about is the way that the visible performance of okay-ness prevents visible grief. The grief becomes private. The grief becomes silent.
