## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: C

The stoic continuation is both a gift and a curse. The gift is that you can function. The curse is that functioning while grieving is profoundly lonely. No one sees that you are broken because you are still showing up. The visible performance of okayness prevents visible grief. The mechanism allows you to survive. But the survival comes at a very high cost.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

Student grief is invisible because you are supposed to keep going. Your grandparent dies and you still go to school. Your future changes and you still do your homework. The world does not pause while you grieve. The mechanism learns to function while broken. You can survive loss without falling apart. But the survival is incomplete. The functioning is hollow. You are here and you are not here. Grief becomes private, silent, something that happens in the spaces between classes and in the night when you are alone.
