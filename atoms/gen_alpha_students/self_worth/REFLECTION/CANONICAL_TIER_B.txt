## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: B

Your entire worth becomes dependent on one thing: your ability to perform well academically. But the performance is not the whole of you. The performance is one small part of who you are. The mechanism is taking one small part and making it the entire person. The fusion is creating a situation where the stakes are always impossibly high. Every grade is not just a grade. Every grade is a judgment about your fundamental worth. You become fused with your performance. Your value becomes your GPA. The mechanism learned this lesson from how you were treated. When you succeeded, the response was pride. When you struggled, the response was disappointment. The mechanism concluded: success equals worth. Now you cannot fail without feeling like you are failing as a person.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
tier_variant: B

Self-worth in students is often directly tied to academic performance. A good grade means you are good. A bad grade means you are bad. The grade is not feedback about one moment of performance. The grade is feedback about who you are as a person. There is a mechanism called identity fusion, and what it does is fuse the grade with the self. The mechanism is not separating the performance from the person. The mechanism is treating the performance as if it is the person. What you come to understand is that the mechanism is learning this fusion from the environment. Parents ask about grades before asking about the day. Schools rank students and communicate rankings constantly. College applications reduce you to numbers. The culture is teaching you that grades are what matters. Grades are what measure your value. The mechanism is preventing you from having a sense of self that is separate from your performance.
