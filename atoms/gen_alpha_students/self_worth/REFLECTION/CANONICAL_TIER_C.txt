## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: C

I have noticed that self-worth in students is often directly tied to academic performance. A good grade means you are good. A bad grade means you are bad. The grade is not feedback about one moment of performance. The grade is feedback about who you are as a person. There is a mechanism called identity fusion, and what it does is fuse the grade with the self. The mechanism is not separating the performance from the person. The mechanism is treating the performance as if it is the person. What I have come to understand is that the mechanism is learning this fusion from the environment. Parents ask about grades before asking about the day.
