I'm calling it bilateral depletion because that's what's happening in your body. You're giving in two directions with no replenishment loop. Your mother calls and needs something. Your daughter calls and needs something. Neither can wait. Neither can be consolidated. Neither allows you to rest between emotional labors. The mechanism operates automatically because the demands don't allow for anything else. The bilateral depletion is not the same as being tired. You can be tired and recover. Bilateral depletion is the state where recovery is impossible because the demands are perpetual and they come from both directions. By the time you've managed one crisis, the other direction is already pressing. There's no gap. There's no space to regenerate. You feel it as a kind of hollowness. Not depression, exactly, though it can look like it. More like the recognition that you have nothing left to give and people keep coming with their hands out. The automatic mechanism in you recognizes this and keeps trying anyway, because the mechanism doesn't have an off switch when the demands are continuous.
