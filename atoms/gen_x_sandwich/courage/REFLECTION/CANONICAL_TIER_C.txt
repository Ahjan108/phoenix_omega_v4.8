I'm calling it the visibility calculation because that's what happens when you're fifty-two and you have something to say but the room is running younger. There's a moment where you feel the impulse to speak. And then there's the quick calculation: if I say this, I'm visible. I'm taking up space. I'm asserting that my perspective matters as much as theirs. The mechanism works automatically to suppress the impulse. The visibility calculation is that moment of decision—except it's not a decision. It's an automatic response baked into your nervous system from years of learning that visibility at your age comes with a cost. Speak up and you become the out-of-touch one. Push your perspective and you become the rigid one.
