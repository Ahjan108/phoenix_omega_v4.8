I'm calling it the midlife flatness because that's what it is. Not the dramatic depression of crisis. Not the sharp pain of acute loss. But a greyness that's been there so long that it feels like personality. The mechanism operates automatically because the flatness doesn't announce itself as a problem. It just announces itself as normal. The midlife flatness is the state where nothing feels quite right and nothing feels quite wrong either. You could be doing something enjoyable and it just feels like you're doing something. You could be with people you care about and it just feels like you're with people. The flatness flattens everything. It makes life into a kind of grey continuation without particular edges.
