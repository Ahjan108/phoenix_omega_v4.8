## REFLECTION v01
---

voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm calling it the provision dependency because that's what it is. Your worth is entirely constructed from what you provide. The mechanism operates automatically to keep you in a position where you're always giving, always providing, always being useful. And the moment that stops—when you retire, when your children don't need you, when your parents pass—the mechanism doesn't know what to do. The provision dependency is not something you consciously chose. It's something you learned early. You learned that your value came from what you could do for others. You learned that being needed was how you stayed connected. You learned that provision was how you proved yourself.
