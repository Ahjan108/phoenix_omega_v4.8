

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're still here."
---
The alarm ran today. It ran during the call. It ran when the reply didn't come. It ran when the to-do list sat open and nothing on it moved. This is what the alarm does when it mistakes urgency for danger. Your nervous system can't distinguish the deadline from the threat. It fires the same response either way. So it ran.
Notice your hands now. Still. The call is over. The reply will come or it won't. The list is there and you are not currently in it. Chest rising. Breath somewhere in your ribs. The alarm ran through the day and the day moved anyway.
What fired this week was not a failure. It was a system doing its job in the wrong conditions.
The system ran. You're still here.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Nobody counts this. You can."
---
Count what the alarm cost this week. Not the large things. The small ones. The email you wrote and deleted. The question you didn't ask in the meeting because the room felt risky. The lunch you ate at your desk because leaving felt like exposure. The conversation you shortened when it asked more of you than you had. These don't show up in any performance review. Nobody tracks the twenty-minute disappearances, the avoidance wrapped in productivity, the energy that went to the alarm instead of the work.
This is worth knowing. Not fixing. Knowing.
Notice where you are sitting. The week is behind you. Not resolved — behind you. These costs were real. They happened. They are also finished.
You didn't spend them carelessly. The alarm spent them. That's a different ledger.
Nobody counts this. You can.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You came back. You always come back."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Weeks of this pattern. Some louder. Some almost quiet. The alarm that ran in the early chapters is running in the same place now. Not because nothing changed. Because this is what patterns do — they return. Not identically. With variation. The way a season returns different from last time and still recognizably itself.
You are not at the beginning. That's not nothing. The alarm runs and you know now what it is running from. That's different from running blind. It doesn't stop the running. It changes the relationship with it.
This is not a better place than the beginning. It is a further place. Further includes more information. More information is not comfort. It is clarity.
Your feet are on the floor right now. That's true every time you land. The floor is always there.
You came back. You always come back.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What it's protecting is the real question."
---
The question this week wasn't whether the alarm was accurate. You know it wasn't fully accurate — not real danger, deadline urgency wearing danger's shape. The deeper question is what the alarm has been protecting. Every alarm protects something. False alarms protect things too. Sometimes they protect the version of you that wasn't allowed to fail in front of the right people. Sometimes they protect a room you were in years ago that felt a lot like this one.
You don't have to answer that tonight. The question just needs to be open. Something is being protected. That's worth holding without forcing.
Notice your breath right now. Somewhere in your chest or your throat. The alarm ran. The question is sitting.
What it's protecting is the real question.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "It isn't only yours. It never was."
---
Someone you know ran the same alarm this week. You don't know that. They looked functional — made it through the all-hands, sent the reply on time, showed up to things. The alarm ran behind everything they did the same way it ran behind what you did. The face that looks like it's managing is the same face you wear. You see it as competence. They see yours the same way.
Nobody says anything. That's part of the system — silence is part of the mechanism. The alarm convinces you that only you are running it. That isolation is one of its costs.
It isn't only yours. It never was.

The action happened while the alarm was still firing. That's what happened. Nothing resolved. The alarm ran through the whole thing. Your body is registering the unresolved — that sensation in your chest or jaw or the shallow place your breath is sitting right now is contact with something real.
That's not failure. That's what incomplete action feels like. The alarm didn't stop you. You moved while it ran. That's the whole job description.
Feet on floor. Breath somewhere. Still in the room.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "It cost more than anyone tracks."
---
The alarm has been running a long time. Not just this week. Years, probably. Count backward: the midnight replies, the meetings you left with your hands shaking, the conversations you shortened, the things you didn't try because the alarm said the cost was too high.
That's what it spent. Not this week. Across time.
You're not here to recover that cost tonight. Just to see it clearly. Seeing it is different from carrying it without knowing what it weighs.
It cost more than anyone tracks.
