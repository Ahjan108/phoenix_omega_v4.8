## STORY v21

path: the feeling without the reason
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
Shame shows up before I understand what I'm ashamed of. I'm in a meeting and the shame is just there. I didn't speak up because I was thinking. But the shame says: you're quiet because you're stupid. I made a small mistake on a document. A mistake anyone would make. The shame says: you're incompetent. The shame doesn't come with evidence. It comes with a feeling. A hot sensation in my chest. A heat in my face. A certainty that I'm not good enough. I'm starting to notice it's not about what actually happened. It's about my interpretation of what happened. Someone criticized my work and I translated it as: I'm bad at my job. Someone said they were busy and couldn't meet and I translated it as: they don't respect me. The shame is the translator. It takes normal events and turns them into proof of my fundamental inadequacy. I notice it when I'm tired. The shame gets louder when my defenses are down. The feeling comes first. The reason comes second. And it never quite fits. The shame is waiting in the wings for the moment I'm weak enough to believe it.
