## STORY v21
---
path: too many people needing me
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I notice it on Tuesday afternoons. The moment when the requests start piling up. My therapist needs me to fill out a form. My mom is asking for advice about her job situation. My friend is going through a breakup. My coworker needs help debugging code. My partner wants to talk about where we're going. And by Tuesday at 3pm, I've run out of space in my head for one more person's needs. The overwhelm is physical. It's a sense of being too small for too many demands. I'm supposed to be available. I'm supposed to be the stable one. I'm supposed to be helpful. And I am. Until I'm not. Until the moment when I realize I have nothing left to give and people are still asking. The overwhelm is the gap between what's being asked of me and what I have to offer. The gap is growing. The asks are constant. My capacity is fixed. The overwhelm happens in the space between them. And the space is getting narrower every day. By the time I reach Tuesday I'm already empty. I'm giving from reserves I don't have.

## STORY v22
---
path: I can't say no
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
There's a part of me that believes I should be infinitely available. I should be the person who always says yes. Who always helps. Who never complains. Who never runs out of energy. The overwhelm comes from the gap between this belief and actual reality. I have limits. I do run out of energy. I do need help sometimes. But admitting any of that feels like failing. So I say yes to everything. I take on projects I don't have time for. I help people even when I'm at my limit. I'm available even when I need to be unavailable. The overwhelm is the cost of always saying yes. The cost of never setting boundaries. The cost of believing my value comes from what I do for others. And I can't afford this cost anymore. But I also can't imagine saying no. Saying no means being selfish. Saying no means not being helpful. Saying no means I'm a bad person. So I stay overwhelmed. I stay drowning in too many asks. I stay believing that my unlimited availability is how I prove my worth. The silence when I imagine saying no is deafening. The boundary I don't set becomes a wall I hit.
---

I'm at the point where I can't think clearly anymore. Every ask feels like an emergency. Every person feels urgent. The overwhelm has scaled up from background noise to complete disorientation. Because stopping means sitting with the reality of what I've become. Which is a person who exists to serve everyone else. A person with no boundaries. A person who is completely overwhelmed and can't stop. The overwhelm is spinning faster and I'm spinning with it and I can't get out. The speed is approaching fatal velocity.

## STORY v24
---
path: I broke under the weight
BAND: 5
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF5
---
I called my therapist and told her I need help. Not to process things. To survive. The overwhelm has broken something in me. I'm having physical symptoms now. My chest hurts. My head hurts. I feel like I'm vibrating at a frequency that will eventually make me fall apart. I told my partner I can't help with anything else. I told my mom I need her to figure out her own problems. I told my boss I can't take on more projects. And then I felt such guilt. Such shame. As if asking for anything for myself is a form of betrayal. The overwhelm didn't just break me. It made me broken in a way that makes me feel bad for being broken. I'm supposed to be strong. I'm supposed to handle all of this. I'm supposed to be the person other people can depend on. And I can't be that person anymore. I'm also terrified of what happens if I'm not that person. Do people leave? Do I lose my value? Do I become irrelevant? The overwhelm has become a crisis and the crisis is forcing a reckoning. I either find a way to set boundaries or I disappear completely. And disappearing completely is starting to feel like the more likely option. The breaking feels inevitable.
