MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal

---
Jordan identifies the watcher as a protection mechanism. The glass went up after the third consecutive month of heavy one-on-ones. His nervous system decided the volume was too much and installed the observation layer as a buffer. The buffer works: he can hold emotional conversations without being depleted by them. ## STORY v21
---
path: noticing suffering first
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I'm someone who sees things. I notice the things in the room that other people don't notice. I notice the person who's quiet. I notice the person whose smile doesn't reach their eyes. I notice the tension. The exhaustion. The things left unsaid. It's a gift. It's also a curse. Because once you notice, you can't unsee. And I notice everything. I see my friend's relationship is dying. I see my coworker is in the process of breaking down. I see my partner is terrified about the future. I see the systems that are crushing people. I see the injustice. I see the suffering. And because I see it, I feel responsible for it. The watcher's burden is that you can't unsee what you've seen. And you're constantly aware of what everyone else is carrying. The compassion fatigue for a watcher is different. It's not from doing. It's from seeing. It's from carrying the weight of all the awareness that other people don't have to carry. The weight of the sight is becoming unbearable. I'm seeing things no one else can see and I'm alone with what I see.
---

## STORY v22
---
path: the weight of knowing
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
There's something lonely about being the person who sees. Everyone seems fine. They're laughing. They're going about their days. And I'm seeing the cracks underneath. I'm seeing the places where they're falling apart. And I'm holding that knowledge alone. I can't tell them what I see because then I'm burdening them with my perception. So I hold it. I see my mom is depressed but she's not ready to hear that. I see my friend is in an unhealthy dynamic but he's not asking for my perspective. I see my team is overextended but the structures won't change. So I watch. I notice. I hold the knowledge of what's really happening underneath the surface. The compassion fatigue of a watcher is this: knowing more than it's safe to know. Seeing more than it's comfortable to see. And having to keep that seeing quiet. The quiet is the heaviest part. The weight of the unsaid observation. The weight of the suffering you notice that you can't fix. I'm drowning in the things I can see but can't speak. The seeing is poisoning me from the inside.

The compassion fatigue is the exhaustion of that position. Knowing and being unable to act. The awareness is a curse. The foresight is useless. The sight is a prison.
