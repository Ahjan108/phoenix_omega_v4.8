Priya sits in a team meeting and watches herself participate. She nods when she is supposed to nod. She speaks when there is a pause. She appears engaged. She is not present. She is watching the version of herself that is being a professional. The watcher is efficient. The watcher knows what to say. The watcher is not Priya. Priya is somewhere behind the watcher, exhausted and empty. The meeting ends and everyone leaves seeming like they were actually there. Priya stands up and walks back to her station and watches herself do it. Her body is functioning. Her consciousness has moved somewhere else. This is the mechanism of the professional watcher: perform the version of yourself that the job requires while the real version of you disappears.

The part that sees I'm wearing the same clothes three days in a row. The part that observes I'm not responding to messages. The part that sees the depression from a distance. The watching is separate from the experience. I'm depressed and I'm also watching the depression. The watcher sees the pattern. The depression creates isolation which creates more depression. The depression creates incapacity which creates failure which creates more depression. The watcher can see the spiral. The watcher understands the mechanism. But the watcher can't stop it. The watcher can only observe. The depression is thick and the watching is from behind glass. I can see what's happening but I can't reach through to change it. The watching is its own form of torture. The knowledge of what's happening without the capacity to change it. The watcher is imprisoned by sight. The sight is the prison.
