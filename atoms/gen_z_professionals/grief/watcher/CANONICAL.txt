Jordan attends his uncle's funeral. He stands with his family. His mother cries. His cousins cry. Jordan watches himself not cry. The watching is specific: he can see the grief in the room, can identify it in everyone around him, but can't find it in himself. It's there—he feels a heaviness, a thickness in his throat—but the feeling can't break through the observation layer. He's watching himself grieve instead of grieving. The watching keeps him upright. The watching also keeps him separate from the room.

## STORY v21
---
path: seeing what's breaking
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I'm watching my friend walk through her father's death and I'm helpless. I see what she's carrying. I see the particular way that grief ages you. I see how she's trying to hold things together while everything is falling apart. And I can't fix it. I can't even really help. I can just watch. The watcher's position in grief is unique. I'm not the one grieving but I'm grieving the effects of the grief on my friend. I'm grieving the version of her that existed before. I'm grieving the fact that she has to go through this. The secondary grief is real. It's watching someone you care about in pain and knowing that pain is necessary and natural and there's no way around it. The watching is heavy. I'm carrying awareness of her suffering. I'm holding the knowledge that she's changed and will never be the way she was. I'm seeing the cracks in her composure when she thinks no one is looking. The compassion is tearing me apart. The helplessness is tearing me apart. And all I can do is watch. The watching is its own form of breaking. I'm breaking because she's breaking.

I see them when they think no one is watching. The moment when the facade cracks. The moment when they're alone in the hallway and their shoulders collapse. As a watcher, I'm carrying the knowledge of the private breaking. I'm holding the awareness that their public face is hiding something much more painful. The watcher's burden is knowing the gap between how they appear and what's actually happening inside. And the knowledge of that gap is its own form of grief. The privacy of their pain is what's breaking me. I know too much about their unwatched moments.

## STORY v24
---
path: the watching has to end
BAND: 5
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF5
---
I need to stop watching. I need to stop carrying her grief. I need to stop being the person who sees and absorbs everyone else's pain. But I can't leave her. Not now. Not when she's falling apart. So I'm stuck. I'm trapped in the watcher position watching someone I love go through something I can't fix. The compassion fatigue of watching grief is profound. You're witnessing something you can't change. You're aware of suffering you can't alleviate. You're helpless in a way that's destroying you. My friend asked me if I was okay and I said yes. But I'm not okay. I'm breaking from watching her break. I'm grieving her grief. I'm losing myself in the mirror of her loss. The watcher is supposed to have perspective. But there's no perspective when you love someone and you're watching them suffer. The only perspective is: this is the worst possible thing. And they have to get through it. And I have to watch them get through it. And we're both changed by this. And I'll carry this forever. The forever is the thing I can't escape.
