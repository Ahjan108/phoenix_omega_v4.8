Jordan maps the shame's accounting system. Credits are discounted. Debits are amplified. A compliment gets a 90% haircut: "They're just being nice." ## STORY v21

path: the failure lives inside

---
There's something fundamentally wrong with me. That's the narrative. The narrative is quiet. It's just in the background. Just the sense that if I try hard enough I can overcome the wrongness. I can be good enough. I can be worthy. But underneath is the belief that the wrongness is real. That I have to earn my place. That I have to prove my value. That my natural state is inadequate and I have to perform adequacy until someone believes me. The shame is in the gap between who I am and who I'm supposed to be. The gap is the evidence. Evidence of my fundamental inadequacy. I'm supposed to be confident. I'm supposed to be ambitious. I'm supposed to want more. I'm supposed to push harder. The shame says this is who I'm supposed to be and I'm not her. The shame is the internal commentary that narrates the gap. The shame is the voice saying: not good enough. Not confident enough. Not ambitious enough. Not enough. The shame lives inside and it's been there so long I thought it was me. Maybe it is me.
