## Mechanism_Proof v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/social_anxiety/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
The mechanism is the split attention itself. Alex speaks and simultaneously evaluates the speaking from an external vantage point. His nervous system has been conditioned to treat social interaction as a threat that requires constant surveillance. The watcher is a survival adaptation — if he monitors himself closely enough, he can prevent failure. But the attention required for internal surveillance is attention unavailable for the interaction itself. The result: his presence becomes hollow. He sounds disengaged because he is partially disengaged — half his consciousness is in the room, half is in the observation booth. The mechanism destabilizes genuine connection because genuine connection requires presence, not performance review.

Jordan's nervous system learned early that social rejection was possible, even probable. The watcher developed as an early warning system. By observing themselves, Jordan could catch mistakes before they happened, could adjust, could prevent the social rupture. The mechanism makes logical sense from a survival perspective. The social anxiety that the watcher was designed to prevent becomes the thing it activates. The mechanism is self-defeating: the effort to appear safe makes the person seem unsafe.
