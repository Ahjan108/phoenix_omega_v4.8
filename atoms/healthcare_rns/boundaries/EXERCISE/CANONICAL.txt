# healthcare_rns × boundaries × Setting and maintaining limits
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Count: 30
# Voice: second-person imperative, somatic, guided
# Spec: 100–300 words, ≤10 words per instruction, explicit counts, no options, one step per sentence
# Persona grounding: break room, car, bathroom, nurses' station, home post-shift

---

## EXERCISE v01

Sit at the break room table after a request.
Place both feet flat on the floor.
Feel where your body meets the seat.
Press down into that contact for five seconds.
Say silently: My body is my own.
Breathe out and stand up slowly.
You have established internal clarity.
Return to your next task.

---

## EXERCISE v02

In the parking lot after shift, sit in car.
Grip the steering wheel with both hands.
Squeeze hard for a count of four.
Release and let your hands rest.
Say silently: I choose what I give.
Repeat this grip-and-release cycle five times.
Your hands have power to say no.
Exit when grounded.

---

## EXERCISE v03

Stand in the bathroom facing the mirror.
Look directly at your own eyes for ten seconds.
Do not look away.
Say silently: I am not responsible for everything.
Splash cold water on your face and neck.
Feel the cold shock.
Pat dry and meet your eyes again.
You are a separate person.

---

## EXERCISE v04

In the break room, sit on the floor.
Cross your legs and sit up tall.
Place your hands on your thighs firmly.
Press your hands down for a count of six.
Say silently: This is where I end.
Release and breathe out slowly.
Repeat this grounding cycle four times.
Your boundaries are physical and real.

## EXERCISE v05

Stand at the nurses' station after family conflict.
Place both feet wide apart on floor.
Press your feet down with your full weight.
Hold this rooted stance for a count of eight.
Say silently: Their need is not my responsibility.
Shift your weight side to side once.
Return to center and feel stable.
You are separate from their emotion.

Repeat this spinal twist cycle six times.
You are wringing out the work of the day.
