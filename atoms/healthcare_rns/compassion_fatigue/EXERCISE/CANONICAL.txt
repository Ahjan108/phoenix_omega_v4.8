# healthcare_rns × compassion_fatigue × Restoring capacity to feel
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Count: 30
# Voice: second-person imperative, somatic, guided
# Spec: 100–300 words, ≤10 words per instruction, explicit counts, no options, one step per sentence
# Persona grounding: break room, car, bathroom, nurses' station, home post-shift

---

## EXERCISE v01

After patient death, sit in break room alone.
Place both feet flat on the floor.
Feel the full weight of your body.
Press down for a count of ten.
Say silently: This weight is real, I am real.
Breathe in through your nose for four.
Breathe out through your mouth for six.
Sit with this feeling for one minute.

---

## EXERCISE v02

In the break room after loss, lie on floor.
Feel your entire back against the ground.
Let the ground hold you completely.
Press your spine down for a count of eight.
Say silently: I can feel this and survive.
Remain on the floor for two minutes.
Notice your breath moving through you.
When ready, roll to one side and sit up.

---

## EXERCISE v03

In your car after a difficult shift.
Sit in the driver's seat facing forward.
Place both hands on your thighs.
Feel the weight of your hands.
Say silently: I felt something real today.
Press your hands down for a count of six.
Release and lift your hands.
Repeat this press-and-release five times.

---

## EXERCISE v04

In the bathroom after shift, stand at sink.
Splash cold water on your face and neck.
Feel the shock of cold sensation.
Let the water run over your wrists.
Cold is alive, and you are alive.
Dry your face with a towel slowly.
Look at yourself in the mirror for ten seconds.
You are still here.

---

## EXERCISE v05

In the break room, sit on the floor.
Wrap your arms around your knees.
Hold yourself tightly for a count of ten.
Say silently: I held someone's pain today.
Release your hold and sit with legs extended.
Feel the floor beneath you.
Sit in silence for one minute.
Your compassion is real and deserves rest.

---

## EXERCISE v06

After witnessing suffering, sit in your car.
Place one hand on your chest.
Feel your heartbeat under your palm.
Say silently: My heart is still beating, still open.
Breathe in through your nose for four.
Breathe out for six.
Feel your heart rate with each breath.
Repeat for two minutes.

## EXERCISE v07

In the break room after loss, stand slowly.
Place both feet wide apart on the floor.
Root yourself to the ground.
Say silently: My body is here even when numb.
Press your feet down for a count of eight.
Feel the earth pushing back against you.
Shift your weight side to side once.
You are still connected.

## EXERCISE v08

At home after shift, sit in dark room.
Let your eyes adjust to the darkness.
Breathe slowly in and out.
Notice the sensation of sitting.
Say silently: I am permitted to rest now.
Sit in darkness for three minutes.
Notice any sensation returning to your body.
Turn on a soft light gradually.

## EXERCISE v09

In the bathroom, run warm water over hands.
Feel the warmth on your skin.
Hold your hands under the warm water.
Say silently: Warmth is real, care is real.
Count to ten while feeling warmth.
Remove your hands and dry them.
Notice the lingering warmth on your skin.
Carry this sensation back with you.

Repeat this four-cycle breathing five times.

## EXERCISE v30

At the end of shift or day.
Stand with both feet on the floor.
Place both hands over your heart.
Say silently: I am still capable of feeling.
Hold this hand-on-heart position for ten seconds.
Press your hands down for a count of five.
Release and let your hands fall.
You are still whole, still human, still here.
