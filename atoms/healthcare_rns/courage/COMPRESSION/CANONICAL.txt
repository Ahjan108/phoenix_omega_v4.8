## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
Courage in healthcare isn't the absence of fear. It's the ability to act in the presence of fear. You speak up about a safety concern even though the doctor is more senior. You advocate for a patient even though you might be perceived as difficult. This is what courage actually looks like in medicine.

compression_family: C2

Courage is expensive in healthcare culture. When you speak up, you risk being labeled as difficult or not a team player. When you set a boundary, you risk being seen as uncommitted. The cost of courage is that it might isolate you from the culture that needs you to be courageous.
