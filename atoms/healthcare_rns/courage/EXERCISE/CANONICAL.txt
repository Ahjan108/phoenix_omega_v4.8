# healthcare_rns × courage × Acting despite fear or doubt
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Count: 30
# Voice: second-person imperative, somatic, guided
# Spec: 100–300 words, ≤10 words per instruction, explicit counts, no options, one step per sentence
# Persona grounding: break room, car, bathroom, nurses' station, home post-shift

---

## EXERCISE v01

Before approaching the attending, stand alone.
Place both feet on the floor.
Press your feet down with full force.
Say silently: My feet are grounded.
Hold this press for a count of five.
Breathe in through your nose for four.
Breathe out through your mouth for six.
You are ready to speak.

---

## EXERCISE v02

In the bathroom before a difficult conversation.
Stand facing the mirror directly.
Look at your own eyes for ten seconds.
Say silently: I have earned my voice.
Nod your head once slowly.
Place your hand on your chest.
Feel your heartbeat for a count of five.
You are physically capable of this moment.

---

## EXERCISE v03

In your car before returning to difficult shift.
Grip the steering wheel at ten and two.
Squeeze hard for a count of six.
Release and place hands in your lap.
Say silently: I can do hard things.
Breathe in for four, hold for two.
Breathe out for six.
Repeat this five times.

---

## EXERCISE v04

In the break room before speaking up.
Stand with feet wide apart on the floor.
Press your feet down equally.
Feel your strength rooted through your legs.
Say silently: I am as valid as anyone here.
Hold this rooted stance for a count of ten.
Take one deep breath in and out.
You have weight and presence.

## EXERCISE v05

Before a confrontation, sit in any chair.
Place both hands on your thighs.
Press your hands down hard for a count of six.
Release and sit with hands resting.
Say silently: I choose what I say.
Stand up slowly and deliberately.
Feel yourself rising.
You are claiming your space.

## EXERCISE v06

In the bathroom, place palms on the counter.
Press your entire upper body weight in.
Say silently: I am substantial and I am heard.
Count to six while pressing.
Release and step back.
Feel the counter supporting you.
Repeat this press cycle five times.
Your voice carries real weight.

## EXERCISE v16

Before approaching a patient to deliver hard news.
Sit in any available seat.
Tense every muscle in your body for four.
Say silently: I can hold this moment.
Release all tension completely.
Breathe in through your nose.
Breathe out through your mouth.
Repeat the tension-and-release cycle three times.
