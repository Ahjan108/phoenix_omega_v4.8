voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: C

I want to name something about courage in nursing that is specific and different from what people usually mean by courage. Courage in most contexts means doing something difficult or dangerous despite fear. Running into a burning building. Standing up to an oppressor. Taking on an obvious enemy. The stories we tell about courage are stories about people overcoming external threats. But the courage required in nursing is often quieter and more confusing because the threat is structural and often invisible. The courage required is to speak truth to power when the power structure has made it clear that truth is not welcome. The mechanism is the collision between what you observe and what the hierarchy tells you to believe.

I want to talk about the specific courage required to say no to an unsafe demand. You are asked to do something you believe is unsafe. A patient ratio that is too high. A medication you do not believe in. A procedure you think is contraindicated. A protocol you know will harm people. And you are asked to do it anyway. The mechanism is the clarity of your own judgment combined with the pressure to comply. You know, with relative certainty, that what you are being asked to do is wrong. And you are being asked to do it by someone with power over you. The courage is in saying no. And the mechanism of the no is important.

I want to end by naming something about what courage actually is in the context of nursing, underneath all of these specific manifestations. Courage is not the absence of fear. Courage is not confidence in the outcome. Courage is not being sure that you are right. Courage is the action taken despite these things. It is the speaking despite the cost. It is the caring despite the risk. It is the trying despite the likelihood of failure. The mechanism is the prioritization of what is right over what is safe. It is the choice to act according to your values even when acting costs you. It is the decision to be authentic even when authenticity is risky.
