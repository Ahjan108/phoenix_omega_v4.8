Nora notices that she is accumulating losses without accumulating the grief. Her patient died. A colleague quit. A family member is sick at home. The unit is short-staffed. The losses are real. ## TURNING_POINT v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nora sits in the grief group at the hospital. Other nurses are talking about the patients they lost. The grief in the room is real and present. Nora is listening and she realizes something: she is grieving right now. Not for her own losses. She is grieving for the losses of the nurses in the room. Their grief is reaching her. Her own grief is not. She sits with this and speaks: "I lost a patient and I couldn't grieve." The room is quiet. One of the nurses says: "That's depression. The grief gets stuck." The naming is the turning point. Her inability to grieve is not a failure of character. It is a symptom. The symptom can be addressed. The grief is still in there. The depression is just preventing access to it.

---
Aisha tells her therapist about the code that failed. The therapist asks: "How did you feel about it?" Aisha answers: "I didn't. I should have, but I didn't." The therapist nods and says: "Your depression is grief-locked. You can't access the grief because the depression is in the way. But the grief is in there. It's waiting for you to be able to reach it." The reframe is significant. The grief is not lost. It is not gone. It is simply not accessible right now. The inaccessibility is temporary. The treating of the depression might open the door to the grief. Aisha leaves the session with the knowledge that her inability to grieve is not about her. It is about the depression's hold on her emotional capacity.
