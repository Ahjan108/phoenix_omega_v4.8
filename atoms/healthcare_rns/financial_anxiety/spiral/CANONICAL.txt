I don't know how it happened so fast. One month I was managing. Not comfortable, but managing. The next month my car needed a transmission repair. Twenty-two hundred dollars. The credit card went on it. The interest rate is twenty-four percent. So the twenty-two hundred is now growing. I'm paying that off as fast as I can and it's only going down by forty dollars a month because the interest is eating most of the payment. Meanwhile my credit union loan has a variable rate and it went up half a percent. My monthly payment increased. Then the hospital reduced shift availability. Budget constraints, they said. So my hours went from forty to thirty-five. My paycheck dropped by eight hundred dollars. Now I can't make the full payments on anything. I'm juggling. I'm paying the minimums and letting the interest accumulate. I'm choosing which bills get paid and which ones get put on credit. This is how the spiral starts. One emergency. One layoff. One rate increase. And suddenly the whole system collapses. I'm watching the math get worse. I'm watching the numbers grow while my ability to manage them shrinks.

## STORY v02
---
path: the cascade of bad decisions made in desperation
BAND: 2
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
I took out a payday loan. I know that's predatory lending. I know it's a trap. The interest is approximately four hundred percent annualized. But I needed four hundred dollars to pay the electric bill so the power wouldn't get shut off. The payday loan is due in two weeks. I don't have four hundred and twenty dollars in two weeks. So I'm going to have to take out another payday loan to pay off the first one. Or I'm going to default. Or I'm going to pay it off and not make my car insurance payment. Each choice leads somewhere bad. This is the spiral. I've entered the space where there is no good decision. There's only degrees of financial disaster. And once you're here, once you've taken the payday loan, the trajectory is set. The payday loan doesn't fix anything. It just moves the crisis forward two weeks. And in those two weeks things don't improve. The hours don't come back. The medical bills don't stop arriving. So by the time the payday loan is due I'm in worse position. I'm now paying interest on top of the debt I already had. I've borrowed against future income that I don't have. And I've put myself in the position where the only way out is to earn significantly more money, which I can't do. I'm stuck. I've made a decision in desperation that has locked me into financial catastrophe.

I sat down with a spreadsheet. I've been avoiding it but I did it. I listed everything I owe. Everything that's due each month. Everything that I'm paying interest on. The total is sixteen thousand three hundred and eighty-seven dollars in consumer debt on top of my student loans. My take-home per month is four thousand two hundred. I have rent at eighteen hundred. Utilities at one hundred fifty. Food at three hundred. Transport at two hundred. Insurance at one hundred fifty. Medications at seventy. That's twenty-eight hundred and seventy. I have one thousand three hundred and thirty left. I have sixteen thousand dollars in debt at various interest rates. The average interest is probably eighteen percent. That's two hundred and forty dollars a month just in interest. That means I'm actually paying two hundred and forty dollars per month to stay in the same amount of debt. And I have only one thousand three hundred thirty left. Even if I put all of it toward debt I'm only paying five hundred dollars down per month. At that rate it will take thirty-two months to pay off the credit card and car loan debt. That's not including the fact that I'll probably need to borrow more to handle emergencies. The math breaks. The system doesn't work. There's no path from here to financial stability that doesn't involve a major life change like a significant salary increase or a major decrease in expenses. Neither of those things are available to me. So I'm spiraling into a position where I'm going to be in debt forever. And the spiral has its own momentum now.

## STORY v04
---
path: the spiral becomes the identity
BAND: 4
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
I've stopped thinking about my future. That's the sign of where this spiral has taken me. I used to plan. I used to think about what comes next. Now I'm just trying to survive the next month. My therapist asked me about my goals and I couldn't answer. Because the only goal is to not crash completely. The financial spiral has become all-consuming. It's the thing I think about every morning. It's the thing that wakes me up at three in the morning in a panic. It's the thing that makes me physically ill when bills arrive. It's the thing that's destroyed my relationships because I don't have the emotional capacity to be present for anyone when I'm drowning financially. I've taken on the identity of someone who's financially failing. That's how I introduce myself now. I'm the nurse who can't afford stuff. I'm the person who has to skip the happy hour because I'm broke. I'm the person who makes excuses about why I can't help with things. I'm defined now by my financial failure. And the spiral has taught me that I deserve this somehow. That I'm not competent enough to get out. That this is my place in the world. The spiral isn't just about money anymore. It's about my belief that I can't change this. It's about my despair that this will be my life until something catastrophic happens.

## STORY v05
---
path: the crisis that doesn't end
BAND: 5
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF5
---
I've been in financial crisis for four years now. At what point does crisis become the baseline? At what point do you stop expecting it to get better and start accepting that this is just how life is? I don't know anymore. The spiral has become so engrained that I can't imagine a different financial reality. I can't imagine not having this anxiety. I can't imagine not checking my bank balance seventeen times a day hoping there's somehow more money than there was yesterday. I can't imagine not doing the mental math of bills versus income and realizing I lose every time. I'm on medications for anxiety. They don't help with the financial anxiety because it's rational. There's an actual logical basis for my panic. I'm actually drowning. I'm actually unsustainable. I actually don't have a way out. So the anxiety is correct. It's accurate. It's the mind's appropriate response to genuine crisis. And it's never going to go away because the crisis never resolves. It just cycles. Emergency, payday loan, temporary relief, larger emergency, deeper hole, repeat. I've become accustomed to the spiral. I've adapted to it. I've accepted it. And that acceptance is the most terrifying thing of all. Because it means I've given up. It means I believe I will be in this spiral forever. And believing that might actually make it true.
