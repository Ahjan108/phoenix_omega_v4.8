COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nora identifies the watcher's migration. At work, the watcher is functional—it allows her to manage multiple patients' emotional experiences without being overwhelmed. At the memorial, the watcher is dysfunctional—it prevents her from grieving a colleague she worked beside for four years. The watcher doesn't have a context switch. It operates identically in both settings because the trigger is the same: proximity to death and grief. The trigger doesn't carry a label—"professional" or "personal." The trigger just fires. The watcher responds to the firing without checking the context. A work death and a personal loss produce the same defensive response. The defense is appropriate in one context and harmful in the other.

Aisha discovers the watcher prevents grief from peaking. Grief needs peaks to process—waves that build, crest, and recede. The watcher intercepts the building phase. Every time the grief begins to build toward a peak, the watcher activates and converts the building wave into observed data. The data is flat. Flat grief doesn't process because processing requires the crest. Without crests, the grief persists at a low, constant, unresolved level. Her cat died three months ago. The grief hasn't moved. It's the same low-grade sadness it was on day one. The sadness should have waves by now. The watcher prevents the waves. The waves are where the healing is.
