path: the body that wakes itself

SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I'm asleep at one in the morning and suddenly my heart rate spikes. It's like someone turned on an alarm inside my chest. I'm awake. My heart is racing. And there's no reason. I wasn't dreaming about anything traumatic. I wasn't in danger. But my nervous system decided that sleeping wasn't safe. My body woke me up with a heart rate of one hundred and ten beats per minute. I check the time. I check my surroundings. Everything is fine. But my body is in fight-or-flight mode. It's prepared for danger that isn't there. This is insomnia caused by hypervigilance. My nervous system has been trained by the job to think that danger could come at any moment. So it wakes me up from deep sleep ready to respond to an emergency that doesn't exist. By the time I've convinced my nervous system that everything is fine, I've been awake for twenty minutes. And then I can't fall back asleep because now I'm thinking about why I woke up. And the thinking keeps me awake. And the insomnia deepens.

So I'm waking up to false alarms constantly. My body is on alert even when there's nothing to be alert about.
