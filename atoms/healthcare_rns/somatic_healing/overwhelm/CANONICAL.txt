## STORY v01
---
path: the body that became the job
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
My back is always tight. My shoulders are always up by my ears. My feet ache before the shift even starts. This is my body on nursing. This is what fifteen years of twelve-hour shifts does to the physical form. I can feel the cumulative strain. The thousands of patient transfers. The hours of standing. The bending and reaching and lifting that the human body was not designed to do eight hours a day. And I'm only forty-two. I'm already feeling like I'm sixty. The overwhelming reality is that my body is being destroyed by the work. And I'm being asked to be okay with it. To ice it on my days off. To stretch. To manage the pain. But nobody's asking me to stop doing the thing that's destroying me. Nobody's asking the hospital to fix the unsafe conditions. To get lifting equipment. To limit the number of transfers. Instead they're asking me to manage the damage. To accept that this is the cost of nursing. And the overwhelm is that I'm aging my body ten years for every year I work here. And I have at least fifteen more years until retirement.

All of these are cumulative injuries from the job. And I'm managing them privately. I'm getting injections. I'm doing physical therapy. I'm taking anti-inflammatories. And nobody at the hospital acknowledges this. They don't ask how my body is holding up. They just ask if I can work the shift. And I say yes because I need the job. But every shift is adding to the damage. Every patient transfer is making something worse. The overwhelming reality is that my body is being sacrificed and nobody cares. It's not even acknowledged as a sacrifice. It's just the job. This is what it costs. And the cost is being paid in cartilage and ligaments and the slow deterioration of the physical form.
