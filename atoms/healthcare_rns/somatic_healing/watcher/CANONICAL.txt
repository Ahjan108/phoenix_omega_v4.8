## STORY v01

path: the body that works while the mind is gone
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
My body is moving through the shift. My hands are doing the work. My legs are carrying me from room to room. My arms are lifting patients. And I'm detached from all of it. I'm watching my body do the labor while I'm somewhere else mentally. My body has learned the job so completely that it doesn't need me to be present. It just does. The physical movements are automatic. The muscle memory is complete. So I'm standing there watching my body work like it's someone else's body. I'm watching my hands chart. I'm watching my feet move. I'm watching the physical labor without being in it. And the detachment means I'm not feeling the pain. Not fully. I'm aware that my back is tight but it's like the awareness is happening to someone else. I'm watching someone's back be tight. I'm watching someone's feet hurt. I'm watching someone's hands get carpal tunnel. And the disconnection is actually helpful because if I were fully present in my body I'd have to feel how much it hurts. I'd have to acknowledge the damage. Instead I'm just watching. Just observing. Just letting the body do what it's learned to do while the consciousness stays separate.

And this dissociation is a survival mechanism. If I felt the full weight of the physical damage I couldn't keep working. So the body has learned to put distance between the pain and the consciousness experiencing it. I'm watching myself limp through a shift like I'm watching someone else limp. I'm observing the body's decline from a place of detachment. And it's both helpful and terrible. Helpful because I can keep going. Terrible because it means I'm not even here for my own body. I'm watching it suffer and I'm not even fully present for that.
