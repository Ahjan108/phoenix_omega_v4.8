## STORY v01

path: the idea of Saturday morning
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
She used to have Saturday mornings. The kind that belonged to her. No schedule. No meetings. Just time to read or walk or sit with coffee and think. Saturday mornings were a thing she could count on. She's noticing the absence of them now. The Saturday mornings still exist as a calendar item, but they're not hers anymore. They're taken by work that didn't fit in the week. Or by the preemptive work for Monday. Or by the mental weight of what's coming. The space where those mornings used to be is just occupied now. This is a small grief. The kind that doesn't have a name. She's not mourning a death. She's mourning the loss of Saturday mornings. The loss of that one thing that was just for her. She thinks about what it felt like to have that time. It's becoming hard to remember. The space where that memory lives is getting dusty. This is what losing things to burnout looks like. Not all at once. Not dramatically. Just the slow erosion of the small things that made life feel like hers.
