## INTEGRATION v01
mode: STILL-HERE
The loop is still happening, but you're not in the middle of it anymore. You're watching it run in the background of your mind, like a browser tab you didn't know you had open. The meeting is over. Your performance was fine. The tape is still playing. And you're noticing the tape instead of becoming it. That noticing—that small separation between you and the thought—is where stillness lives. You're here in your body, in the moment that's actually happening. The loop is still running, but it's not occupying all the space anymore.

## INTEGRATION v02
mode: COST-VISIBLE
You spent an hour thinking about something that took fifteen minutes to do. The hour of analysis produced zero new insights, zero improvement opportunities, zero forward movement. You did better with the actual action than you did with the thinking about it. This is visible to you now. You can see the math that's broken. You can see the cost. And knowing the cost is different from knowing the loop exists. You can't unknow the cost. That changes whether you're willing to pay it next time.

## INTEGRATION v03
mode: BODY-LANDED
The tension you carry around the thing you're overthinking is not just in your mind. It's in your shoulders, your jaw, your stomach. When you let the thought go, your body knows it before your mind does. You feel the release. The point is not to never have the thought again. The point is to notice when your body is holding the tension of a thought that no longer needs to be held. Let your body be the teacher. It knows when you're done.

## INTEGRATION v04
mode: QUESTION-OPEN
Instead of "Did I do it right?" which your brain will litigate forever, ask instead: "What can I learn from how this went?" Or: "What would I do differently next time?" Or: "Is there something I want to adjust for the future?" These are questions with answers. The original question—"Did I do it right?"—doesn't have one. It only has arguments. The new questions let you move forward. You don't have to close them completely. You just have to move through them.

---
## INTEGRATION v05
mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
What would you tell a friend who was doing what you're doing? You'd probably tell her: "You did fine. You're being too hard on yourself. Let it go." You have access to that wisdom about others. The work is learning to have it about yourself too. Not as a forced positivity move, but as an actual acknowledgment that the standard you're applying to yourself is harsher than the one you'd apply to someone else. That asymmetry is the problem. Noticing it is the beginning.

## INTEGRATION v06
mode: STILL-HERE
The thing you're worried about—that you missed something, that you weren't enough, that it will eventually be discovered—is not happening right now. Right now, you're sitting somewhere, breathing, being. The worry is about a future that hasn't happened and a past that can't change. The only moment where you have actual agency is this one. And in this one, you're okay. The loop tries to convince you that you need to solve everything through thinking. You don't. You just need to be here.

## INTEGRATION v07
mode: COST-VISIBLE
Every minute spent in the loop is a minute not spent on something that actually matters to you. Not just work, but rest, connection, creativity, the things that make you feel like yourself. The loop is stealing your life in small increments. You probably wouldn't trade an hour of rest for an hour of analysis that produces nothing. Yet you do this trade every week. Seeing that you're making this trade—that's the moment when you can stop.
