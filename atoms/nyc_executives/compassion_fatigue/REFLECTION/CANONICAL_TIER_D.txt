tier_variant: D

The mechanism I am thinking about is the sustained outward redirect of internal resources. In a leadership role, you are managing people. You are holding their uncertainty. You are responding to their needs. You are taking on their problems as problems you are responsible for solving. This is good leadership. It is also extraction. The person being managed is receiving support, attention, problem-solving capacity. The person managing is expending these things. And there is no symmetrical refill.

I have been thinking about the difference between short-term depletion and chronic depletion. In short-term depletion, you are temporarily overwhelmed. But you know that the intense period will end. You know that rest is coming. You know that capacity will be restored. In this state, you can still function. The light at the end of the tunnel keeps you moving. Chronic depletion is different. It is not a temporary state. It is the baseline. You wake up depleted.
