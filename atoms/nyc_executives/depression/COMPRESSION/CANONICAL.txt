## COMPRESSION v01
---
compression_family: C1
---
The mechanism of depression in high performers is that it arrives disguised as pragmatism. You are not sad. You are realistic. You are not unmotivated. You are seeing clearly that nothing matters as much as you thought it did. This is the trap. This is how depression sells itself to intelligent people. It comes as clarity instead of illness. It comes as honesty instead of neurochemistry. By the time you realize something is wrong, you have already adopted its logic.

---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
compression_family: C2
---
The cost of the relentless climb is that you arrive at the top and feel nothing. This is not defeat. This is depression. You have reached the place you were trying to reach and the relief you expected is not there. The wins feel hollow. The status feels empty. What should feel like victory feels like a con you have been running on yourself. The cost is the slow discovery that the goal was never the real goal.

---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
compression_family: C3
---
Your body does not lie. When depression arrives, it comes as a heaviness that sleep does not touch. Your movements slow. Your voice lowers. The effort required to do the simplest thing increases exponentially. This is not laziness. This is what depression does to the nervous system. It dampens the signal. It makes everything require more energy. Your body is giving you information. It is telling you that something is fundamentally wrong with the way you are living.

---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
compression_family: C4
---
The relational consequence is that you have become unreachable to the people who care about you. They cannot land on you with their affection. You cannot receive it. It bounces off a surface that you have made impermeable. They interpret this as rejection. Actually, it is a symptom. You cannot let them in because there is no "in" anymore. The capacity to connect has been switched off. This isolation is not chosen. It is the structure of the depression itself.

---

## COMPRESSION v05
---
compression_family: C5
---
The pattern of depression is that it does not announce itself with drama. It arrives gradually, like a room slowly losing light. First you do not notice it is darker. Then you do not notice that you are moving more slowly. Then you do not notice that you have stopped moving at all. By the time the darkness is complete, it feels like the natural state of things. You have forgotten that light is possible.

## COMPRESSION v06
---
compression_family: C1
---
What depression does to the executive is that it makes you doubt everything you know about yourself. You were the person who moved fast. Now movement is impossible. You were the person who had all the answers. Now everything seems uncertain. You interpret this as personal failure. Actually, it is neurochemistry. The person you were did not disappear. The system that held that person in place has been destabilized. The person is still there. The system is broken.

## COMPRESSION v07
---
compression_family: C2
---
The hidden cost is the slow erosion of your sense of purpose. You begin to wonder if any of this was ever real. The goals you worked toward seem arbitrary. The people you thought were friends seem transactional. The meaning you built your life around seems thin. You do not realize this is depression rewriting your narrative. You think it is honesty. You think you are finally seeing clearly. Actually, you are seeing through a filter of neurochemistry that distorts everything toward meaninglessness.

## COMPRESSION v08
---
compression_family: C3
---
Your brain chemistry has shifted in ways you cannot see but your body can feel. The neurotransmitters that fuel motivation and pleasure have been depleted or misallocated. This is not a character flaw. This is biology. Your body is not betraying you. It is trying to tell you that something in the system needs to change. The depression is a message. You have been running the system too hard and the system is shutting down to protect you.

## COMPRESSION v09
---
compression_family: C4
---
The relational fact is that you have made yourself unreachable because reaching you requires a vulnerability you no longer have access to. The people around you do not know what to do with your withdrawal. They try to fix it. They try to cheer you up. They try to convince you that life is worth living. None of this lands because depression has cut you off from the part of you that could be reached. You are alone with an illness that thrives on isolation.

## COMPRESSION v10

compression_family: C5

The recursive pattern is that depression makes it harder to seek help because seeking help requires admitting that something is wrong and admitting that something is wrong requires a kind of vulnerability that depression has made impossible. So you hide it. You perform normalcy. You show up and do the work and go home and shut down. The hiding requires energy you do not have. The deception compounds the isolation. The isolation deepens the depression.

## COMPRESSION v14
