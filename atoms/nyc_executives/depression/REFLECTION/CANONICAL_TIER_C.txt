tier_variant: C

There is a particular form of depression that happens in high-performers. It is not the obvious kind. It is the functional kind. The kind that exists underneath the outputs that continue to be delivered. The person is still working. Still producing. Still appearing to be the same person they were before. The external evidence suggests continuity. The deal closes. The team continues. The results are delivered. But internally, something has gone offline. The engagement that used to be there is diminished. The energy that used to be available is reduced. The sense of meaning that used to accompany the work is diminished. The mechanism is the performance-continuation masking. And the shift is not going back on its own.

I have noticed a particular quality of depression in executives. It is the depression of having gotten to where you wanted to get and finding that it is not what you thought it would be. You worked toward this. This position. This compensation. This status. You achieved it. And now you are here. And it is not what you thought it would be. This can produce a specific kind of hopelessness. Because if getting here did not produce what you thought it would produce, what will. If the thing you worked for is not it, then what is it. The mechanism is the destination-arrival disappointment. The person is left with the question: if not this, then what.
