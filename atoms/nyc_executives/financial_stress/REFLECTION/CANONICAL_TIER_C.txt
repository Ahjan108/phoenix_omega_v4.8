## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: C

I have come to understand a particular paradox of high compensation. The more you earn, the more financially anxious you can become. This seems counterintuitive. More income should mean more security. But the mechanism is different. As your income rises, your lifestyle expands to match it. The compensation becomes the baseline. Anything below the baseline feels like deprivation. I noticed this first when my compensation increased significantly. The anxiety did not decrease. It shifted. The concern was no longer about whether I could cover basics. The concern became about whether I could maintain the lifestyle that my compensation had become. The mechanism is the lifestyle-expansion tracking. As income rises, expenses rise proportionally. The baseline of comfort expands. The anxiety persists.
