There is a particular way that grief can be invisible in a high-performance environment. The person experiences loss. But the person continues to produce. Continues to show up. Continues to deliver. The loss is real. But it is absorbed into the continuation. The grief does not interrupt the work. The person is functioning while grieving. The grief is running in a separate track. The mechanism is the parallel-track operation. The person is simultaneously in grief and in production. Two separate systems running at the same time. The grief does not stop the production. The production does not stop the grief. The grief persists because there is no space for it to be processed fully.
