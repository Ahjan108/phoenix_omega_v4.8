The Slack notification just hit. My nervous system already spiked before I could read it. A message from my director. The spike happens first—the adrenaline, the tightness in my chest—and then I read the message. It says: "Great work on the quarterly review, very solid foundation." Relief. I've conditioned myself to threat-detect on everything, which means I'm always in a state of pre-emptive panic. By the time the content arrives, I'm too flooded to process it clearly.

My director is probably going to stop sending me good news in Slack because getting positive feedback shouldn't cause someone to have a panic response.
