## STORY v01
---
path: giving_from_an_empty_tank
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I have nothing left to give anyone. My partner needs support. My kids need presence. My coworker needs me to cover her shift. My parent is having a health crisis. And I'm empty. Completely empty. The ask to care about anyone else's suffering when I can barely keep my own head above water is impossible. The overwhelm is that everyone else's needs arrived simultaneously and I'm supposed to have resources I actually don't possess.
---

## STORY v02
---
path: the_accumulation_of_other_people's_pain
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
I've absorbed: my daughter's anxiety about a friendship, my partner's stress about work, my friend's depression, my mother's loneliness, my coworker's health scare. Each one lodged itself in me. I'm a repository for everyone else's difficult emotions and now I'm so full of their pain that I can't access my own. The overwhelm is carrying bodies full of grief that isn't even mine.
---

## STORY v03
---
path: needing_care_and_giving_anyway
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
I'm depleted. I'm also the person who can't admit that, because someone will need something from me immediately. So I'm simultaneously drowning and holding others afloat. The overwhelm is that I'm supposed to care when I'm empty, supposed to have presence when I'm fragmented.

## STORY v04
---
path: the_impossible_triage
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
Everyone needs something simultaneously. I have to choose who gets my last bit of energy and that choice is impossible. Someone will be disappointed. Someone will need something I can't give. The overwhelm is real—I actually cannot meet all the needs in front of me. Something will break. Likely me.
