## STORY v01
---
path: the_loss_that_never_ends
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I lost time with my kids to survival mode. I lost connection with my partner to exhaustion. I lost myself somewhere in the logistics of keeping everyone alive. These losses accumulate and there's no funeral for them, no ceremony. The grief is quiet and constant and unspoken.

## STORY v02
---
path: mourning_the_parent_i_wanted_to_be
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
She was going to be patient. She was going to have presence. She was going to feel connected to her children in a way that mattered. That parent is dead. I killed her with my ambition and my need to be responsible. The grief of that is deep.

## STORY v03
---
path: the_childhood_i_survived,_revisited
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
I'm giving my kids a better life than I had. I'm also giving them my trauma, repackaged in modern problems. My childhood was chaotic; theirs is fragmented but scheduled. I'm grieving that I couldn't break the cycle the way I wanted. I'm repeating it in a different key.
---

## STORY v04
---
path: grief_for_time_that's_passed
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
My oldest is going to be a teenager soon. The gap between who I've been as a parent and who I meant to be is large and fixed now. I can't get that time back. I'm grieving the relationship I didn't build because I was too tired. The grief is complicated because I also love who she is and what we have is real. But the loss is still there.
