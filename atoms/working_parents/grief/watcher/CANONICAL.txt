## STORY v01
---
path: observing_my_own_mourning
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I'm watching myself grieve in slow motion. From a distance, I can see the sadness that I can't fully access when I'm in it. The watcher recognizes the grief—the loss of the person I thought I'd be, the parent I intended to be. But the observer is separate from that grief, which somehow makes it both more clear and more sad.
---

## STORY v02
---
path: the_distance_allows_recognition
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
Because I'm watching instead of drowning, I can see what I'm losing. My presence with my kids. My attention to my partner. My sense of self. The watcher notes these losses in a way the person living them cannot. It's a peculiar form of grief—mourning from outside the thing I'm mourning.
---

---

## STORY v03
---
path: witnessing_my_own_withdrawal
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
I can see myself pulling away from people I love. I can see the walls going up. The watcher recognizes this as a grief response—I'm protecting myself from further loss by losing first, by withdrawing, by choosing the distance. The recognition is sad and also somehow inevitable.
---

## STORY v04
---
path: outside_my_own_sorrow
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
The watcher is experiencing the grief one step removed. She's sad about the sadness. She's mourning the person who should be mourning more fully. It's a strange doubling, a grief about grief, and it's lonely in a specific way.
