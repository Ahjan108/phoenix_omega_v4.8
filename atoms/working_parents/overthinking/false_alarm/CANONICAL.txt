## STORY v01
---
path: the_email_that_wasn't
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I read it again. Then again. The tone feels off. My boss said 'let's sync up tomorrow morning'—does that mean trouble? Am I being pulled into a meeting because of something I said in Slack? I panic-scan my recent messages. Did I overstep? Was I unclear? My heart rate climbs for twenty minutes before my colleague IMs: 'did you see the all-hands about the restructuring?' Ah. Not about me. But my body took an hour to believe it.

## STORY v02
---
path: small_tone_in_the_text
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
My partner writes: 'okay.' Just that. Usually it's 'OK!' or 'sounds good!' or an emoji. That period feels heavy. I've convinced myself by the time I get home that they're upset about something, that I've done something wrong. We have a careful conversation. Everything is fine. They were just typing quickly. But for three hours I was already writing apologies in my head.
---

## STORY v03
---
path: the_silent_kid
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
My daughter is quiet in the car. Usually she narrates her entire day. Not today. She's staring out the window. Something's wrong. Is she being bullied? Is she struggling with the math? I construct crises in my head while driving. At dinner, she's just tired. Tired. That's all. But I've already activated every worry protocol.
---

## STORY v04
---
path: the_missed_call_notification
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
A call from school, missed. My blood stops. I call back immediately—my mind has already written the story: injury, behavioral incident, problem. The secretary answers cheerfully: they just wanted to confirm tomorrow's field trip. The relief is physical, but so is the humiliation at how fast I escalated.
