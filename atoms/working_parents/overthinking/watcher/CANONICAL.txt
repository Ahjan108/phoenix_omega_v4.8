## STORY v01
---
path: watching_myself_panic
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I can see myself from outside. There I am, standing in the kitchen, hands shaking slightly while I pretend to be fine. My daughter is asking me something and I'm nodding but I'm not really here. Part of me is watching this performance—the smile that doesn't reach all the way, the way I'm gripping the counter. I'm noticing my own numbness while trying to feel present. The watcher part of me catalogs each failure: there's the forced cheerfulness, there's the way I flinched when my partner mentioned the budget.

## STORY v02
---
path: behind_glass
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
Everything I'm experiencing feels like it's happening behind glass. I'm going through the motions—making breakfast, responding to emails, helping with homework—but there's a layer between me and it all. I can see myself being a good parent, being competent, being enough. But I'm watching from somewhere far away. It's like observing an actor playing my role. She's doing well, that woman at the stove. I just can't feel her.
---

## STORY v03
---
path: the_overhead_view
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
If I zoom out far enough, I can see myself as a small figure moving through rooms: working at the desk, driving, cooking, folding laundry. There's a kind of relief in that distance. Up here, my anxiety is small. My failures are just pixels. But then I have to come back down into my body and the distance collapses.

## STORY v04
---
path: emotional_autopilot
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
I'm piloting myself through the day. The part of me that feels things is quiet—or muffled, or very far away. My son hugs me and I hug back with the right pressure, the right timing. My voice sounds warm and present. I'm doing it correctly. But the watcher in me notes that I felt nothing. Or almost nothing. A ghost of what should have been there.
