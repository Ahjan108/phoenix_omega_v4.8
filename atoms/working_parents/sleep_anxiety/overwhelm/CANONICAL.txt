## STORY v01
---
path: too_much_to_hold_at_once
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
My brain won't settle. There's the work presentation, the permission slip I forgot, whether I should call the pediatrician about the cough, my mother's health, the leak in the guest bathroom, the awkward conversation with my partner, the guilt about the screen time I allowed. It's all happening at the same time in my head at 11 PM and there's too much. Sleep requires stillness and my mind is a traffic jam.

## STORY v02
---
path: the_capacity_ceiling
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
By the time I get to bed, I'm saturated. The day has filled me completely. There's no room for calm or rest—I'm packed with obligations, worries, decisions, and the accumulated fatigue from holding everyone else's needs first. Sleep should be a release but I'm too full. Nothing can settle.

## STORY v03
---
path: holding_too_many_threads
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
I'm trying to keep track of everything at once and it's like juggling in the dark. I lie awake tracking which ball might drop first. Did I respond to that email? Did I write down the appointment? Did my kid's teacher get my message? The threads multiply as I lie there and I can't let any of them fall.
---

## STORY v04

BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4

There's always someone who might need something tonight. I'm half-awake even when asleep, one ear on the monitor, one part of my brain on-call. I can't fully rest because I can't fully stop working. The weight of being the person who keeps things together keeps me vertical even horizontally.
