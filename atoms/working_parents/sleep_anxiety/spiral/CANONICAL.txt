## STORY v01
---
path: the_insomnia_loop
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I can't sleep because I'm anxious about not sleeping. Tomorrow is going to be awful if I don't sleep. This awful tomorrow makes sleep impossible. I've now been awake for three hours thinking about how much worse everything will be. My exhaustion spirals into desperation spirals into accepting that I won't sleep which somehow makes it worse.

## STORY v02
---
path: calculating_backwards
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
If I fall asleep right now, I'll get 4 hours and 17 minutes. Is that enough? If I fall asleep in five minutes, 4 hours and 12 minutes. I'm losing time by thinking about time. Each minute I'm awake makes tomorrow worse, which spirals my anxiety, which makes sleep harder. The math is vicious and circular.
---

## STORY v03
---
path: catastrophizing_the_exhaustion
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
I won't sleep. Tomorrow I'll be a bad parent—impatient, sharp-tongued, distracted. I'll make a mistake at work. I'll be unsafe driving. This is spiraling into a story of how one night of bad sleep ruins everything. By the time I'm imagining myself having caused an accident, sleep is functionally impossible.
---

## STORY v04
---
path: the_spiral_of_focus
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
The more I try to sleep, the more awake I am. The more awake I am, the more I focus on sleeping. My effort becomes my resistance. I spiral into frustration with my own mind, which somehow makes everything worse. By midnight I'm not just awake, I'm angry at myself for being awake, which is another problem my brain won't let go of.
