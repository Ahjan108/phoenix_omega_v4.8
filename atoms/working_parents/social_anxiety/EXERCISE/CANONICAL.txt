# working_parents × social_anxiety × the performance that never turns off
# Slot type: EXERCISE
# Count: 30
# Notes: step-based (Step 1/2/3)
---
## EXERCISE v01
Step 1: Before the social situation, pause. Notice where anxiety lives in your body—chest, throat, stomach. Name it without judgment.

Step 2: Ground yourself in three concrete facts: the temperature of the room, the light quality, the number of people. These are real. You are real.

Step 3: Make one choice for the first two minutes—ask one question or listen without performing. Remove the pressure to be perfect.
---
## EXERCISE v02
Step 1: After the conversation, write one thing you're replaying. Just one sentence.

Step 2: Ask yourself: Would I judge someone else for saying this? Be honest. Most of us are harsher with ourselves than anyone else.

Step 3: Challenge the fear with evidence. Has this fear been true before? What actually happened?
---
## EXERCISE v03
Step 1: List the specific fears about the social situation. Not general ones. The specific things that could go wrong.

Step 2: For each fear, ask: Is this based on what actually happened before, or on what I'm afraid might happen?

Step 3: Counter each fear with evidence from times when you were anxious and it was fine anyway.
---
## EXERCISE v04
Step 1: Notice when you're replaying something. The moment you catch yourself, pause.

Step 2: Ask: Did the conversation end? Do people still respect you? Am I the only one remembering this?

Step 3: Replace the replay with a memory of a time you felt easy in a social moment. Not perfect. Just safe.
---
## EXERCISE v05
Step 1: Commit to one small authenticity before the social situation. Say one thing that's true instead of performed.

Step 2: In the situation, when you catch yourself performing, pause and ask: What do I actually think? Say that.

Step 3: Notice afterward: Did being slightly more real make things worse or better?
---
## EXERCISE v06
Step 1: Identify the room or situation you're avoiding. Get specific about what you're afraid will happen.

Step 2: Imagine being in the room anyway. Not succeeding perfectly. Just being present.

Step 3: Do something small this week in that direction. One small step toward the avoided situation.
