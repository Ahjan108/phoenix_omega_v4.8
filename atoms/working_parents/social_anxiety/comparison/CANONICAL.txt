## STORY v01
---
path: the_instagram_parent
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
My former college friend posts a photo of an elaborately plated breakfast she made for her kids. She's also a parent. Why does her house look like that? Why are her children's clothes matching? She has the same 24 hours I do and yet somehow she's made sourdough and arranged flowers. The comparison breeds a kind of low-level resentment and also genuine confusion about what I'm doing wrong.

## STORY v02
---
path: the_work_friend_without_kids
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
She stays late. She leaves at 6:01 for drinks. She has the mental and physical space to be excellent at her job. I'm trying to do the same job with a fraction of her available focus. I watch her be promoted and wonder if the difference is just that she doesn't have to partition her brain. The comparison is that she's not managing multiple people's basic needs and it shows.

## STORY v03
---
path: the_partner_who_could_be_helping
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
My partner works too but somehow I'm the one who remembers the dentist appointments, who knows what everyone's wearing, who's coordinating schedules. They're not doing less; they're doing a different less. I compare my mental load to theirs and the comparison is so unfair I have to look away.

## STORY v04

BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
