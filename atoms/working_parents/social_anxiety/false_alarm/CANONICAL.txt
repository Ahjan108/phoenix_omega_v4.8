path: the_group_chat_misread
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I send a joke in the group chat. Nobody responds for an hour. Did I miss the tone? Was it mean? Now I'm rereading it obsessively, convinced I've offended someone. By the time someone responds with a laugh-emoji, I've already written an apology in my head and decided I'm the kind of person who says the wrong thing.

## STORY v02
---
path: the_parent-teacher_conference_echo
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
The teacher said my daughter is 'spirited.' I heard: difficult. For a week I'm convinced there was something critical in that word, some coded message about my parenting. I replay the five-minute conversation repeatedly, reconstructing intent. Finally I text another parent: 'did your kid's teacher say anything about [daughter]?' and learn she uses 'spirited' for kids she likes. The relief is real. The embarrassment lingers.

## STORY v03
---
path: the_work_comment_that_haunts
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
In the meeting, someone didn't laugh at my comment. I said something intended to be light and it landed flat. I've replayed it fifty times. Was I trying too hard? Did I miss the cultural reference? Am I now that person? For three days I'm certain everyone noticed and judged me. Probably nobody did.

## STORY v04
---
path: the_invitation_that_might_not_be
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
A work friend mentions a happy hour 'with the team.' Does that include me? Why wasn't I explicitly invited? I spend the afternoon in a low-grade panic about whether I've been silently excluded. Then I realize nobody explicitly invites me to anything—we all just show up. My anxiety invented a rejection that doesn't exist.
