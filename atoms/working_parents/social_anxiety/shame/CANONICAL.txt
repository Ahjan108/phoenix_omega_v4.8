## STORY v01
---
path: the_parent_i'm_not
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
I see another mom at pickup—she looks composed, together, like she's not coming off a work call and a blown deadline and the realization she forgot to pack vegetables. My kids are louder than hers. My hair is still in its 7 AM bun. I feel exposed as the person who's struggling, who doesn't have it together the way she does. The shame is that I think everyone can see what I'm failing at.
---

## STORY v02
---
path: the_professional_me,_divided
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
At work I'm competent. At home I'm barely managing. And I'm terrified that someone at work will find out how much I'm struggling with basic things—that I sometimes feed my kids whatever's fast, that I haven't mopped the floors in weeks, that I sometimes yell. If they knew, they'd know I'm not really the person I perform as. The shame is that there's a gap between who I pretend to be and who I actually am.
---

## STORY v03
---
path: the_admission_i_can't_make
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
My friend says she's having a rough time. I want to tell her about my roughness. But I can't say it. There's a shame in admitting I'm not okay, that I'm drowning sometimes, that I'm angry at my family for existing and needing me. So I say 'me too, but we're doing okay' and the isolation of that partial truth is shameful in itself.
---

## STORY v04
---
path: the_comparison_i_lose
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
Other parents have help. Cleaners, partners who are actually present, more flexible jobs. I'm doing this on fumes and no backup. There's shame in needing help and not having it, in being less equipped than everyone else seems to be. The shame is that I want what I don't have and that wanting is somehow a failure.
