## STORY v01
---
path: every_system_at_once
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
---
My shoulders are tight, my stomach is in a knot, my breath is shallow, and there's a low-level vibration through my whole body like I'm standing on something that's about to shift. It's all happening simultaneously and I can't isolate one thing to address. The overwhelm is somatic—it's bigger than my nervous system can hold. My body is full.

## STORY v02
---
path: the_tension_with_nowhere_to_go
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
---
I need to move, to shake it off, but I also need to sit still at my desk. I need to scream, but I need to smile at the kids. The tension has no outlet. It builds with no release, so it somatically accumulates—in my jaw, my back, my stomach. By evening my whole body is a clenched fist.
---

## STORY v03
---
path: contracted_on_every_level
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3
---
I notice my body is small. I'm taking up less space, breathing less deeply, moving more carefully. Physically I'm trying to be smaller so I require less—less attention, less resources, less of everything. The overwhelm has me in a protective crouch even when there's no danger.
---

## STORY v04
---
path: the_body_keeps_score
BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
---
I realize that every day for months, my body has been in a low-level emergency response. The cumulative dysregulation has become my baseline. My nervous system doesn't remember how to be calm. The overwhelm isn't a moment; it's a condition my body lives in.
