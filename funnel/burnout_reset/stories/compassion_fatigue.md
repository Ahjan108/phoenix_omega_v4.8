## compassion_fatigue_social_worker_story

**emotional_intensity_band:** 3
**story_origin:** composite
**story_type:** character_study

**Before:** Tanya carries forty-two active cases. At night she dreams their stories, not hers. She holds the warm coffee cup every morning but does not taste it. Her body runs on someone else's fuel. Her supervisor asks how she is doing. Fine, she says. The word means nothing.

**Practice:** She tries five senses grounding between home visits. Five things she sees. Four she hears. Three she touches. Two she smells. One she tastes. She does it in her car with the engine running. The leather seat. The air freshener. Her own pulse in her wrist.

**After:** She tastes her coffee the next morning. Bitter and slightly burnt. Tears come, which surprises her. It is the first thing she registers as hers in months. Not a case. Not a file. Hers.
