## sleep_anxiety_parent_story

**emotional_intensity_band:** 2
**story_origin:** composite
**story_type:** character_study

**Before:** The baby sleeps through the night for two months. Nadia does not. She lies awake listening for sounds that are not there. Her fingers grip the monitor until the screen burns into her retinas. She snaps at her partner in the morning. Not about the baby. About the coffee. She knows it is not about the coffee.

**Practice:** She tries coherence breathing after putting the baby down. Six-second inhale, six-second exhale. She uses the rhythm of the white noise machine as a pacer. Her grip loosens. She breathes with the machine instead of against it.

**After:** She falls asleep before the third round on the fourth night. The monitor stays on. But she stops gripping it. Her hands rest open on the pillow. The vigilance had a shape. It was a fist.
