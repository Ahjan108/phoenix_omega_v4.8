## somatic_healing_athlete_story

**emotional_intensity_band:** 2
**story_origin:** composite
**story_type:** character_study

**Before:** Mei retires from competitive swimming two years ago. Her shoulders still seize every morning as if she is about to race. Her physiotherapist says there is nothing structurally wrong. Her body disagrees. She sleeps on her back because turning onto her side triggers the brace. The race is over. Her shoulders have not been informed.

**Practice:** She tries body scan breathing on her living room floor. Slow inhale, move attention to her shoulders. Exhale and notice without forcing. She stays with the tension instead of stretching past it. The tension does not vanish. But it stops recruiting her whole back.

**After:** On the eighth day the seizure does not come. Not because the muscle changes. Because her nervous system stops bracing for a race that is over. She sleeps on her side for the first time in two years. The body let go of something the mind forgot it was holding.
